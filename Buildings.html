Buildings are the third level of world development, requiring both districts and specializations to be constructed. Buildings may be ruined by orbital bombardment or collateral damage. Buildings that are ruined can be repaired for half of their cost and construction time.

Building sets

Each building belongs to one or more building sets. A building can only be built in a district that permits one of its building sets and does not exclude any of them, which depends on its specialization. Changing the specialization of a district may change what buildings are supported and the new specialization's tooltip will display which existing buildings will be retained and which ones will be removed.

  • The five general building slots available on all planets, as well as the Urban Expansion specialization, can accept most building sets. The main exceptions are extraction buildings, which can only be built in relevant extraction districts.
  • Most specializations can accept an automation building, which produces extra workforce. There are several types with different building sets, each of which has a planet limit of 1. They affect all jobs of types created by the district and its buildings, even if created by other districts or their buildings.
  • The Civilian Industry specialization can accept buildings relevant to production of consumer goods, as well as rare resource refineries. Similarly the Heavy Industry specialization can accept buildings relevant to production of alloys. Mixed Industry can accept both.
  • Research specializations can accept research buildings relevant to their specialization (all of them if it is a generic Research Enclave). Archives can accept both research and unity buildings, whereas Administrative Hubs can only accept unity buildings.
  • Resource districts can accept relevant resource buildings, including output boosters such as the Energy Grid. Notably, Bio-Reactors are considered agriculture buildings, since they alter Farmer jobs to produce energy. Some specializations can accept others, for example Mineral Research Support (mining district specialization) can also accept an Applied Mechanics Institute.
    • Agrarian Idyll empires can build Agrarian Villages specializations which can accept any "urban" buildings, including energy and mineral extraction.

A total of 21 buildings may be built on a planet or Nomadic Arkship. By default the primary district specialization always contains six building slots. Resource districts allow an additional three building slots per specialization.

Capital buildings

Main article: Planet capital

The capital always occupies the first building slot and provides some of the basic housing, amenities, defense armies, jobs that reduce crime, as well as other jobs which vary depending on empire authority and civics. Capital buildings cannot be demolished, downgraded, disabled, or ruined by orbital bombardment or collateral damage, and are automatically converted to the appropriate type if the planet is traded, ceded, or conquered by another empire with a different authority or if the planet designation is changed.

Unique buildings

Main articles: Planet unique buildings, Empire unique buildings

Unique buildings are buildings that can only be built a limited number of times per planet or empire, and typically provide effects beyond the creation of jobs. Many of them require the capital to have reached a certain level.

Housing buildings

Housing buildings come in two levels and increase Housing and Amenities. They can be build in all specializations that allow government, urban, entertainment and resort building sets as long as the planet is not a Penal Colony or Thrall-World and are converted if the world is conquered by an empire that uses a different building line.

Fallen empire buildings

Fallen empire buildings are the most efficient buildings in the game. Each one takes 600 days to build or upgrade. They are not converted if the world is conquered by an empire lacking the technology to build them and can also be repaired if ruined.

There are four ways an empire can get access to fallen empire building technologies:

  • If Apocalypse is enabled, empires with the Enigmatic Engineering ascension perk can draw the technologies as research options. (Up to 4 total researched.)
  • If Ancient Relics is enabled, using the Reverse-Engineer Arcane Technology artifact action has a 20% chance (40% with the Archaeo-Engineers ascension perk) to grant the ability to build a random fallen empire building once.
  • If The Machine Age is enabled, empires with the Cosmogenesis ascension perk who have reached the required ambition level can draw the technologies as research options.
  • If BioGenesis is enabled, one of the tasks assigned by the Shattered Fragment fallen empires can have a random fallen empire technology as a reward.

Empires with the Cosmogenesis ascension perk that accepted the Keepers of Knowledge demand to end the crisis path will no longer be able to build fallen empire buildings, even if they unlocked them another way.

Empires with the Wilderness origin must pay an additional 400 Biomass to build or upgrade a fallen empire building.
