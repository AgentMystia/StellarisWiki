Enclaves are special AI empires. They do not own any planets or systems, but consist of large stations. Once an empire has made contact with an enclave, they can purchase various services from the enclave. Enclaves are listed in the Diplomacy Menu under the "Others" category. Each enclave has two or three basic interactions, and a number of restricted interactions that can only be used after sufficient Opinion is reached.

The maximum Opinion with an enclave is 100, and an empire builds it by using their services or owning the system with the enclave. Enclaves in owned systems gain +1 Opinion with the system owner each year. This is not cumulative when owning multiple systems with the same enclave.

When an enclave modifier expires, the empire will be offered the option to extend it.

Genocidal empires cannot interact with enclaves.

Shroudwalkers

Available only with the Overlord or Shadows of the Shroud DLC enabled.

The Shroudwalker Enclave is an enclave dedicated to harnessing the power of the Shroud and spread across up to three stations. The systems with these stations will be connected via Shroud Tunnels. On larger galaxies, one of the systems might be protected by a Psionic Avatar. Empires with the Teachers of the Shroud origin will start in contact with the Shroudwalker Enclave.

If either an endgame crisis takes place or an empire with the Galactic Nemesis ascension perk reaches ambition level 5, the enclave will disappear. Empires with the Teachers of the Shroud origin will receive 50 Zro and a choice between +10% monthly Influence or Unity for 20 years.

The Shroudwalkers are in opposition with the Mindwardens and will refuse to communicate with empires that have 100 Opinion with Mindwardens enclave or have the Mindwardens origin. Each time an empire gains Opinion with Mindwardens, it will lose an equal amount with Shroudwalkers. Constructing a Shroud Seal for the first time will also reduce the Opinion of Shroudwalkers by −5.

Mercenary enclaves

Available only with the Overlord DLC enabled.

Unlike other enclaves, mercenary enclaves do not exist at the start of the game and instead are founded during gameplay. Gestalt Consciousness empires cannot found mercenary enclaves unless they have the Tactical Algorithms civic, but they can become patrons by taking ownership of an enclave's system. Fanatic Purifiers and Mindwardens empires cannot found mercenary enclaves.

Founding a mercenary enclave requires sufficient mercenary enclave capacity, a fleet of size 50 or larger that cannot cloak led by a Commander in an owned system with a moon or asteroid, and no other enclave already present in that system. Founding a mercenary enclave costs 100 Influence, 2000 Alloys, and either 1500 Energy if the empire uses a mechanical shipset or 1500 Food if the empire uses a biological shipset. Fleets containing space fauna cannot found an enclave. Once the order is given, the enclave constructs its station in the fleet's current system, the fleet's commander leaves the empire to become the leader of the new enclave, and the fleet is transferred to the enclave. The enclave will start with 30 Opinion towards its patron.

Mercenary enclaves provide the following services:

  1. Empires can rent a mercenary fleet for 10 years and gain +15 opinion for an Energy cost that scales with the enclave's opinion and tier. The contract can be renewed at any point for an additional energy cost and +5 opinion. Mercenary fleets are led by Commanders with the Mercenary Warrior trait. If the mercenary enclave station is attacked, all mercenary fleets are recalled, and their clients are refunded between 3000-44000 energy, depending on enclave tier.
  2. Empires that have at least 20 opinion with the enclave can pay 600, 1100, or 2000 energy to hire 5, 10 or 15 mercenary armies. If the enclave is at least tier 4, empires can also pay 1200 or 2300 energy to hire 5 or 10 mercenary mechanized armies. All options will grant opinion equal to the number of armies hired, plus 5 additional opinion if hiring a mechanized army.
  3. Empires that have at least 30 opinion with the enclave can buy +15% naval capacity for 10 years. This costs between 1000 and 5000 energy based on current capacity and gives +15 opinion.
  4. Empires that have at least 40 opinion with the enclave can pay 1000 energy to unlock the Mercenary Garrisons and gain +15 opinion.
  5. Empires that have at least 50 opinion with the enclave can pay 1500 or 5000 energy to gain +15% damage versus a mid-game or end-game crisis.

Mindwardens

Mindwardens
Available only with the Shadows of the Shroud DLC enabled.

The Mindwarden Enclave is an enclave dedicated to fighting against species with the Psionic or Latent Psionic traits and such species will not be able to interact with the enclave. Their system contains a Shroud Seal megastructure. If an empire with the Mindwardens origin exists, the enclave will not be created at the start of the game but can be created by the empire with the origin 2 years after they defeat the starting psionic empire. If the enclave was created at galaxy generation, it will use the Mindwarden shipset. If it was created by an empire, it will use the same shipset as the empire.

The Mindwardens are in opposition with the Shroudwalkers and will refuse to communicate with empires that have 100 Opinion with Shroudwalkers enclave. Each time an empire gains Opinion with Shroudwalkers, it will lose an equal amount with Mindwardens.

The Mindwardens will contact any empire that encounters a Psionic Aura and their founder species does not have the Psionic or Latent Psionic traits.

If the Mindwardens were created at the start of the game and are destroyed, they will respawn in 10 years in a random unowned system. If they are destroyed a second time, they will not respawn.

Attacking enclaves

While enclaves never become hostile to any empire except in retaliation, they can be attacked like any neutral entity. With around 16k fleet power and highest level weapons they are no easy prey but various rewards can be salvaged from their remnants, depending on the type of enclave destroyed. If an enclave is attacked it will cancel all ongoing deals with the attacking empire and if the enclave is destroyed it will cancel all ongoing deals with all empires. Enclaves regenerate outside combat.

Founding enclaves

Available only with the Overlord or Shadows of the Shroud DLC enabled.

Empires that do not have the Mindwardens origin can found mercenary enclaves while empires that have the origin and defeated the empire in the cluster behind their home system will unlock in two years the option to found a mindwarden enclave. Founding an enclave requires sufficient Enclave Capacity and a fleet of size 100 or larger that cannot cloak and is led by a Commander in an owned system with no other enclave present. Founding an enclave costs 100 Influence, 2000 Alloys, and either 1500 Energy if the empire uses a Mechanical shipset or 1500 Food if the empire uses a Biological shipset. Fleets containing space fauna cannot found an enclave. Once the order is given, the enclave constructs its station in the fleet's current system, the fleet's Commander leaves the empire to become the leader of the new enclave, and the fleet is transferred to the enclave.

  • Mercenary enclaves require a moon or asteroid in the system and will start with 30 Opinion.
  • Mindwarden enclaves can be founded in any system and will start with 100 Opinion. After founding an enclave the empire can offer every year to pay 1000 Energy to the enclave to increase their capacity by 1.

Enclaves founded by empires build and upgrade ships the same way playable empires do, using all technologies researched by the empire that founded the enclave at the time of the founding. The enclave's patron can grant the enclave all its researched technologies and gain +1000 Energy and +15 Opinion.

The empire that owns an enclave's system, initially its founder, is the enclave's patron. Patrons have slightly reduced prices and receive a dividend at regular intervals. The dividend interval is mechanically a situation, and the base progress rate is +0.417 per month, resulting in a total time of 20 years. With all positive modifiers maxed out, the interval is a mere 1.83 years, though that relies on a non-patron hiring the fleet. More consistently, with the patron hiring the fleet, the minimum interval is 2.25 years. The rate is increased (and thus total interval decreased) by the following:

Crystalline empire

Available only with the Federations DLC enabled.

If playing with the Void Dwellers origin (AI empires can't trigger it), then there is a 1% chance that during the mid-game, a unique enclave known as the Crystalline Empire spawns near the galactic core. Its system does not have hyperlane connections but instead has a wormhole to a random system in the galaxy. Empires that are not Genocidal and have not attacked them can offer to help them every 20 years to receive one of the following:

  • 1 use of the Settle Crystalline Refugees decision on a Habitat for 10 years pops)
  • 1 use of the Crystalline Construction decision on a Habitat for 10 years
  • A level 7 scientist

Destroying the Crystalline Empire enclave grants no rewards.
