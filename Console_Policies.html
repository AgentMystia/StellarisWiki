Version

This article has been verified for the current console version (3.14) of the game.
See also: Species rights

Policies outline the stance of an empire on other empires, treatment of pops, and acceptable behavior. Once altered, a specific policy cannot be changed until 10 years have passed. Some resolutions in the Galactic Community can make certain policies a breach of galactic law. In this case the policy will show a warning sign next to it.

Changing policies is advised as a way to mitigate faction unhappiness in large empires that have a lot of pops of different ethics, besides the possibility of opening up new possible avenues of power.

Orbital Surrender Acceptance

Orbital Surrender Acceptance determines whether surrenders caused by orbital bombardment are accepted. AI empires will always accept surrenders. This policy is not available to Genocidal empires as planets will never surrender to them. Bombarding a world for the first time will bring the option to set the initial policy, which cannot be changed for at least 10 years.

Type Effects Description
Prohibited Surrenders are not accepted
There will be no surrender. Orbital Bombardment will continue indefinitely.

This policy becomes available once the Positronic AI or Artificial Administration technology is obtained, or after contact with certain empires. This policy is not available to Gestalt Consciousness empires. AI empires decide this policy based on AI personality.

Gestalt Consciousness empires cannot access this policy, but may give the Slave Citizenship without it (except Rogue Servitors, who must subject them to Mandatory Pampering instead, and genocidal types, who purge them).

Purge

Purge policy determines an empire's position on the forced removal of species that are no longer wanted. This policy option only dictates which degree of purging is allowed; further details on which pops are to be purged and in what way can be managed with the species rights system. Pops that cannot integrate with the empire (e.g. Hive-Minded pops outside a Hive Mind) may be purged regardless of this policy setting.
