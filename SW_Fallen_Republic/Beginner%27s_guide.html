Introduction

Welcome to the Star Wars: Fallen Republic Mod! We hape you enjoy your time playing. Due to this mod beeing a Total Conversion certain aspects of the Game are diffrent to how they are in the basic Stellaris experience. This page aims to point out these diffrences and help guide the Player through the new challanges approching him during his playtime. This however is not a guide on how to play Stellaris, basic knowledge on how to play is recommended.

Empire Selection

This mod has pre defined Empires based on Canon and Legends knowledge of the many Space faring Nations of the Star Wars Universe. These can not be edited and have, should the Player choose any of the Canon Maps, a pre set starting location with the lore accurate Homeworlds and Traits as well as Civics fitting to them. These can be seen in detail in the Empires tab. Assuming a lore start these Empires have vastly diffrent starting strengths with diffrent possibilities. The Galactic Empire will start the Strongest with a lot of the Core Worlds, an exclusive Federation Type and the ability to build the Deathstar.

Galaxy Generation

The Player can choose between multiple different Maps. The Star of the Show beeing the Canon Map with accurate Planet positions according to known Maps and Planets belonging to the correct factions as of the end of Episode III. This Map can be choosen in two smaller versions for more game performance as well as a Mode where everyone starts with just one Planet in the typical Stellaris fashion, but with all the Planets of the Star Wars Galaxy. Should you choose the Sandbox mode it is recommended to move the mid- as well as endgame dates several years back, as Empires will develop much more slowly. An overview of all possible starts can be found here. It is recommended to play on the Large Canon Map for the Full Star Wars: Fallen Republic Experience.

Economy

The Economy of Fallen Republic is vastly different from the one currently found in Stellaris, it is somewhat similar for those who have played 2.7 or previous interations after 2.0. In general it is recommended to build designated Worlds with a sole production focus and boosting these with the Planetary Designations, an example of that would be a Forge world that only does not build more Foundries in order to provide amenities or housing. It is possible to inform oneself of all the districts appearing in the mod in detail here.

Regular Worlds

It's not possible to build Industry Districts, however one can build multiple Foundries in order to create large volumes of Alloys or Consumer Goods, which are only limited by the amount of free Buildslots a players Planet has. The Number of these slots is much more then in the base game. Normal planets are important producers of Basic Goods, but fall short in Terms of Production output of Ecumenopoli.

Ecumenopolis

In the mod an Ecumenopolis provides basic resources as well as having larger quantities of production output modifers. The Planetary Metropolis Designation is recommneded to choose. It is advised to always upgrade planets to Ecumenopoli if possible as they improve the Planetary Output massively while having no major downside.

Specialization districts

In the mod it is possible to research new distric types that can be created on planets. When clicking on the Planetary Decisions Tab these can be choosen to be allocated to a planet. On every Planet you may only choose between two of these. But can choose so on every Planet again. These Districts then Scale with the Regular Planetary Size like any other District. This makes it possible to make highly specialized and productive Planets with singular purposes, a Research World is a good example for that. In general the same District will provide a greater Bonus on Ecumenopoli then on a regular world making these highly desirable to choose first.

It is noteworth that these Districts also add +5 to the buildable district amount making just doing these in order to gain more minerals or other ressources a viable option.

Ressources

There are a few new Ressources that come into play when playing this mod. Additionally Rare Ressources of the Base game are mostly gone. An overview of all those in the mod can be found here.

Ordanance, Starfighter Parts, Plasma- and Laser Cells

are all essential Components in Starship Manufacturing, however a lot of those are most often not needed and having one or two factories of each is most often enought production for an empire.

Hyperfuel

is what all ships run on. It is the major Bottleneck in regards of building a bigger Fleet and should always be taken into consideration before buying high market prices. It first needs to be mined and then refined, especially mining is difficult because its sources are rare. It is advised to research Synthetic Hyperfuel production if possible.

Bacta

is one of two ways to base medical treatments on, not having enought will debuff the Army mostly, but is generally not advised to do. It can be found either minable in space or as a random planetary deposit distributed at the start of a game.

Military

Combat behaves very diffrently starting with very diffrent weapons systems and interactions between each other. Its generally adivised to build mixed fleets of Missle Ships, Carriers, Battleships and Point Defense Ships.

Building Ships

Corvetts and Civilian Space ships can be contructed at Starbases, any larger Vessels need to be build at Speical Shipyards that can either be in a Planetary Orbit or a Planetary Ring.

Ship Designs

Depending on the choosen starting Empire the Player will have unique, lore fitting Ship Designs that consist of own aspects and armaments to them. It is not possible to switch between them, so the Rebels can not build Star Destroyers.

Research

All techs have been completely overhault and are unique to the Mod. The Player should look out for Starship Structure Upgrades as these grand the Ability to Research newer, bigger and better Starships.
