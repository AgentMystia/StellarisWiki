Species traits

Species traits represent a species' innate functions, abilities, and personality. Most traits come in mutually exclusive groups that affect the same aspect of the species, such as Nomadic and Sedentary. These groups include traits with opposite effects, as well as stronger variants of a trait. Though traits are initially decided at a species level, individual pops of the same species can end up with different traits with genetic modification, producing new subspecies. Some traits affect how strongly a pop is attracted to certain factions. Hovering over a species' portrait will list the combined effects of all species traits.

Biological traits

Main article: Biological traits

Each organic species starts with a set of biological traits that can be selected at empire creation. Biological species traits can changed through genetic modification, which requires the Gene Tailoring technology.

Machine traits

Main article: Machine traits

Each robotic species can be modified with a set of machine traits. If playing as a machine empire, machine traits can be selected at empire creation, and later changed through machine modification. Organic empires and machine empires with the Life-Seeded, Voidforged or Shattered Ring require the Machine Template System technology to change machine traits.

Pop modification

The template modification menu

A species' traits are selected at the start of a new game but those can be changed or improved as new technologies are researched. Both organic and robotic pops can be modified and each has its own set of traits. In addition, a small number of events can modify the population of a colony.

Species with the Clone Soldier or Clone Soldier Ascendant traits cannot be genetically modified.

Number of traits

The number of traits that a species can have is limited by two values: trait points and trait picks. Additional trait points and trait picks can be obtained throughout the game from various sources.

  • Trait Points are spent on positive traits and added by negative traits. Traits that have both positive and negative effects usually do not cost points, nor do certain special traits. The starting number of trait points is 2 for biological species, 1 for Machine species, and 0 for Mechanical species.
  • Trait Picks is the total number of traits that a species may have. The starting number of Trait Picks is 5 for biological and Machine species and 4 for Mechanical species. Traits which do not cost Trait Points also do not cost Trait Picks.

Species templates

Population modification is done via templates, which creates a subspecies. Templates can be applied to pops per subspecies (including original species), per planet, or per subspecies on a planet. Modified pops are considered part of the same species as the original even if the name and portraits have been changed. They will inherit the same species rights as the original species but those can be then changed to something different for each subspecies.

Templates with zero current pops can be freely modified or deleted[1]. However, templates with at least one pop cannot be changed directly and the template modification window presents only the option to create a new one.

After a template has been applied, a special project to modify the selected pops appears in the situation log. The project is canceled in 30 days if not started. Depending on the type of modification, how severe the modification (total amount of trait point differences), and how many pops were picked, this special project can be very expensive in science. The system is currently designed towards favoring bulk modification of many traits over specific via a high base cost.

The cost to modify a species can be reduced by −50% by the following traditions: Synthetics tradition tree finisher, Advanced Tools, Modular Cybernetics or Allelic Expression. Additionally, the Plentiful Fossils archaeology site can add a permanent −25% modification cost.

Auto-modding

An auto-modded pop

Certain traits grant the auto-modding effect, which will cause pops with the trait to take on the bonuses of another trait that's beneficial to their job. Every month a pop on every world will be auto-modded, the number is increased by 2 for every level of pop assembly building. The effects of auto-modding are not shown in the Species menu but can be seen if a pop is selected in the Population tab.

There are four auto-modding traits, each with its own set of traits it can simulate.

Uplifting

For pre-sapient species, the option to create templates is replaced with the option to uplift and bring them to sapience. It requires the Epigenetic Triggers technology and costs 3000 Society research. Upon completion, the empire instantly integrates the uplifted species and gains 50 Influence. In addition, the uplifted species gains +10% Happiness when living within the borders of the empire that uplifted it. The initial ethics of the uplifted species are determined by a series of initial events. Afterwards, the species is fully susceptible to ethics divergence.

Ascension paths

Available only with the Utopia, Synthetic Dawn, or The Machine Age DLC enabled.

Certain species traits can be added or unlocked by a number of ascension tradition trees.

  • Genetic ascension focuses on genetic modification. It grants additional trait points and trait picks, unlocks advanced genetic traits and relaxes restrictions on adding and removing traits.
  • Psionic ascension adds the Psionic trait, which can be added to other species through assimilation.
  • Cybernetic ascension adds the Cybernetic trait, which can be added to other species through assimilation, and unlocks cyborg traits.
  • Synthetic ascension transforms organic pops into robotic pops, improves robotic pops, and potentially unlocks advanced robotic traits.

Available only with The Machine Age DLC:

  • Modularity ascension focuses on robotic pop modification. It grants additional trait points and trait picks and unlocks advanced robotic traits.
  • Virtuality ascension adds the Virtual trait, which cannot be acquired or removed through pop modification.

Hybrid species

Xenophile empires can pick the Xeno-Compatibilty ascension perk to allow the reproduction of two biological species into a hybrid species. Hybrid species take a portrait from one of the parent species and a mix of traits from both parent species, with at least one trait being inherited from each. They also get +1 trait point and +1 trait pick. Two species are 3 times more likely to create a hybrid species if they are part of the same species group. Hybrid species cannot create further hybrid species. They also cannot have traits that are mutually-exclusive.

Some events related to Caravaneers or overlords can also create hybrid species.
