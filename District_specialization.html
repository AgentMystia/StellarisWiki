Specialization is the second level of world development, requiring districts and unlocking buildings. Most specializations add three building slots to the world and additional jobs for each district present. However specializing a district will reduce its housing. Specializations can be replaced at any time and the new specialization's tooltip will display which existing buildings will be retained and which ones will be removed. The tooltip will also display the approximate output and upkeep of all jobs produced by the specialization.

Each primary district can have two specializations while each secondary district can have one specialization.

Adding or replacing a district specialization takes a year. On most planets and megastructures this costs 1000 Minerals while on arkships it costs 500 Alloys.

Specializations slots of the primary district may have certain requirements to unlock:

  • On planets, the second specialization of the primary district requires a tier II planet capital.
  • On Habitats, the second specialization of the primary district requires the Habitat Expansion technology, Void Dwellers origin if the habitat is the empire capital, or the Functional Architecture or Constructobot civic.
  • On Relic Worlds with the Collapsed Spire blocker, the blocker must be cleared to unlock the first specialization slot. The first specialization is automatically set to Central Spire when the blocker is cleared.
  • On arkships, district specialization is always available.

The amount of jobs added and housing subtracted per district is multiplied by the following:

  • ×1.5 if the planet is a Habitat and the empire has the Void Dwellers origin or the Voidborne ascension perk
  • ×3 if the planet is an Ecumenopolis, Hive World or
    Machine World
    Machine World
    Nanite World
  • ×5 if the planet is a Ring World
  • ×2 if the district is a secondary urban district
