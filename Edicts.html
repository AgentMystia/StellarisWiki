Empire edicts window

Edicts are empire-wide decisions that are enacted by spending resources or paying a monthly upkeep. Most edicts are unavailable at the beginning of the game and must be unlocked through research or events. Edicts are automatically canceled if the empire runs out of the required resource.

Unlike other edicts Damn the Consequences ignores Edict Fund and has an upkeep equal to 80% of the empire's Unity income. Once activated, the edict cannot be canceled for 5 years.

Sacrifices

Available only with the Necroids DLC enabled.

Sacrifices are edicts available only to empires with the Death Cult or Corporate Death Cult civic who have at least 100 worked Mortal Initiate jobs or a subject with at least 100 worked Overlord Mortal Initiate jobs. All sacrifices will kill all pops holding the jobs, with the edict granting benefits scaling with what percentage of the population that had Mortal Initiate jobs (max 15%). In addition to its specific effects, each sacrifice grants +5% Pop Growth Speed and +1.5 Unity per 100 Death Priests. Only one Sacrifice edict can be active at any time. Unlike other edicts, sacrifices do not have an upkeep cost but only an upfront cost, and their bonus lasts for 5 years. The small empire multiplier is 0.64 with less than 5000 Pops, 0.8 with less than 10000 Pops, and 1 otherwise. The random multiplier is the following:

  • 16.6% chance for 15
  • 66.6% chance for 20
  • 16.6% chance for 25

The final multiplier is small empire multiplier times the random multiplier times the ratio of Mortal Initiates to Pops and then round to closest 0.1. The maximum possible multiplier is 3.75 with small empire multiplier at 1, random multiplier at 25, and ratio of Mortal Initiates at 15% or more and final multiplier is 3.8 after round to closest 0.1.

Sacrifice: Bounty will also add +1 Monthly Organic Pop Growth to Reassigner jobs if the empire has the Permanent Employment civic.
