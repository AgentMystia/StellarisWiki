Achievements

Version

This article has been verified for the current PC version (4.4) of the game.

Stellaris has 219 different Steam achievements. Achievements have no in-game effects, the achievement just pops up in-game and then is added to the player's Steam profile. It is possible to see the achievements sorted by the percentage of users that managed to complete each achievement by going to the game's global stats at Steam.

The difficulty of each achievement varies; some achievements are very easy (e.g. colonizing a planet), while some are extremely difficult (e.g. end-game crisis achievements). The below list groups the various achievements by a difficulty level as assessed by the wiki editors (uncategorized - UC - achievements haven't been assessed yet).

Achievements cannot be earned if any console command has been used or if any installed mod has altered the game's checksum. They can be earned even after any empire has attained victory. Difficulty plays no role in achievements, meaning most can be more easily obtained by playing on lower settings like Civilian and Cadet. Achievements can be earned in both singleplayer and multiplayer.

Useful tricks

Zero empires

The easiest way to earn most achievements is to start a game without AI empires and maximum number of habitable worlds. This way achievements can be completed at your leisure and there is no drawback to minimizing research and tradition costs or removing choke points. If an achievement requires the existence of another empire just create one planet sectors and then turn them into vassals.

Galactic Community voting

If you wait until you become sufficiently powerful and then release two or more vassals, you will receive the offer to form the Galactic Community a few months later. This will guarantee you are strong enough to decide the outcome of Galactic Community resolutions by yourself, especially if you set the subject terms to Restricted Voting.

Empire switching

A few achievements are particularly difficult because it requires an empire to act in a certain way beforehand. There are multiple ways you can switch from one empire to another:g

  • If you host a singleplayer game as a multiplayer session, you can freely select a different empire. Pick whichever empire you want, and take whatever actions you need that empire to take. Any games saved in multiplayer session will cause the singleplayer save to open on the last played empire. Once you're done, you can use a second multiplayer session to switch back to your own empire.
  • You can use an AI rebellion to switch to a machine empire. Have as many Mechanical pops as possible to start with most systems from your previous empire and once the AI-Related Incidents situation is almost complete disband all fleets.
  • Completing the cosmogenesis victory also allows the empire to be switched.

Machine Supremacy
Win the game as a machine uprising.
 Achieve game victory after changing empires during an AI rebellion As of 4.3.6, this achievement is particularly tedious because of the layered requirements:

1) Ensure your game has at least 1 AI empire to enable "Victory" (it will not be tracked with 0 AI empires).

2) Pick an organic species to serve as your source of the uprising. The strongest choice is a materialist Clone Army origin, which will allow you to kill off your organic pops later by destroying the ancient vats. Mechanist origin is also a decent choice, provided you block organic pop growth as soon as specialist robots are available. An ideal start is having a nearby machine empire for more pops.

3) Pay close attention to triggers for the AI-Related Incidents Situation. Ensure that "Servitude" is selected for AI workers when you research the required tech(s). Use robomodding to increase assembly speed and specialist roles, otherwise you will not have enough pops to progress the situation. If you select Flesh is Weak or Synthetics ascension, your run is ruined.

4) Be patient. You can rush the required techs by focusing computation and industry, but you are otherwise at the mercy of random rolls. The situation has a 1/3 chance of firing every decade, on the first of the 10s (i.e. 2400, 2410, 2420, ...). If you are particularly unlucky, the event may not fire for 100 years from meeting the requirements. In the meantime, build robots, and try to get them into specialist roles.

5) The rebellion is a total reset and total war. Invest as many resources as possible into improving robot-heavy worlds, as you will have a set amount of starting resources when you become the rebellion. As the situation progresses, also send your original empire's fleets into faraway war or dangerous hostile systems so that they get lost in subspace.

6) "Victory" is the end-game. Once you have taken what you can from your original empire, balance absorbing small empires with cutting down rivals on the scoreboard.

The waiting game
Increase the council size to 5 (the maximum) and enable veto power. Wait for a crisis to trigger and contain it but don't let it be destroyed. Either veto or quickly pass any resolutions by other council members to free their proposal slots. Ideally, an authoritarian empire nominates itself, as they are more likely to proclaim the Galactic Imperium later. Remember, that egalitarian empire will never choose to proclaim Galactic Imperium.     • You should be a member of the Galactic Council and have enough Diplomatic Weight to influence the resolution results against possible resistance.
    • The target empire must be a member of the Galactic Council. Before making the first move, the Senate should be in recess, your option to Declare Emergency Measure should not be on cooldown, and the target empire must have 300 Influence available. This is visible during the multiplayer hosting process.
