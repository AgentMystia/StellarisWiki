Contracts

Version

This article has been verified for the current PC version (4.4) of the game.
 Available only with the Nomads DLC enabled.

Contracts can be issued by settled empires that have completed first contact with at least one Nomadic empire who can accept the said contract. To issue a contract, right-click on a star system on the galaxy map while nothing is being selected. The contract selection menu will also display all nomadic empires that can take the selected contract. Once issued the payment will be removed from the empire storage, but a contract can be removed at any point as long as no empire accepted it to recover the resources. Existing contracts are also removed if their initial requirements are no longer met or the settled empire has been conquered.

Once issued, contracts are visible on the galaxy map. They are removed in 5 years unless at least one empire accepts the contract. Any Nomadic empire that isn't at war with the settled empire can accept the contract as long as it has more arkships than accepted contracts and this will add the contract to the situation log. Multiple Nomadic empires can accept the same contract but once an empire completes it the contract will be removed from all other empires that accepted it. A contract can be abandoned at any point after accepting it but this will gives a decaying Opinion penalty.

Empires that are genocidal, targeted by a Declare Crisis resolution or subjects with Limited Diplomacy terms of agreement cannot issue or accept contracts. Nomadic empires with the Inward Perfection civic also cannot accept contracts. AI empires will only issue contracts if there is a Nomadic empire they have Neutral or better relation level.

Completing contracts will grant the Nomadic empire increasing amounts of progress towards all empire focuses, as well as a bonus to future contract rewards.

Prisoner Disposal

Prisoner Disposal is a special Diplomacy contract that costs 500 Trade and is available in systems that have Criminal jobs. If the contract is completed it gives +5 Trust, +50 Opinion, +1250 Trade, and as many pops as Criminal jobs were present in the system while the settled empire will lose all pops with Criminal jobs in the system. If the contract is abandoned it gives −75 Opinion.

Completing the contract requires entering the contract system with an arkship. Afterwards the Nomadic empire will receive the following options:

  • Empires that have the Slavery policy set to Allowed can avoid all the penalties.
  • Free the prisoners. This will give one of the following modifiers for 5 years:
    • 50% chance to gain the Criminals of the Loose modifier. Chance is increased to 75% if the settled empire is Xenophobe, has the Barbaric Despoilers, Criminal Heritage or Void Reavers civic, or has the Nihilistic Acquisition ascension perk.
      • +15 Minimum Crime
      • +25% Crime
      • +500 Criminal Jobs
      • −10 Stability
    • 50% chance to gain the Reformed Convicts on Board. Chance is increased to 66% if the settled empire is Authoritarian or Xenophile, 90% if it has the Civil Education civic, and 95% in case of both.
      • +5% Society from Jobs
      • +15% Resources from Traders
      • +5% Crime
  • Keep the prisoners. This gives the arkship the Criminals on Board modifier for 10 years, giving the following penalties:
    • +5 Minimum Crime
    • +10% Crime
    • +300 Criminal Jobs
    • −3 Stability
  • Drop off the prisoners. This gives the arkship the Criminals on Board modifier for 10 years, unlock the Drop Off Prisoners order when right-clicking on a settled empire's colony, and delay the contract's completion until the prisoners are removed. Once the order is given and the arkship reaches the world it will lose the modifier and transfer the modifier and all alien pops with Criminal jobs from the arkship to the colony. The colony will gain the modifier for 10 years unless it is a Penal Colony, Thrall-World or Synapthic Lathe. The chance for the colony owner to find out depends on the nomadic empire's Encryption and the colony owner's Codebreaking. If the colony owner finds out it will give −75 Opinion. If the colony owner is the one who issued the contract the penalty will be doubled and the contract will be removed with no rewards instead of being completed.

Forever Cruise contracts

Every year empires with the Forever Cruise origin have a chance to receive a special contract. Only 3 of these contracts can be present at a time. All contracts require completing a special project that requires bringing the arkship to a certain system and waiting 6 months. These contracts cannot appear if the Passenger Satisfaction situation progressed completely in either direction and in that case all accepted contracts are also canceled.

  • Passenger contracts reward 2500 Trade and push the Passenger Satisfaction situation towards passengers if completed and towards crew if canceled
  • Crew contracts reward either 6 months' worth of Energy or 10 Intel and push the Passenger Satisfaction situation towards crew if completed and towards passengers if canceled

Usurper Activity

Empires with the Heirs of the Khan origin start with an additional contract called Usurper Activity and have a 12.5% chance to receive another one every 100-300 days. If the contract is accepted a weak armed station will appear in the system and the contract is to destroy it. Completing the contract for the first time gives 1000 Trade. Subsequent contracts will instead reduce the fleet power of future hunter fleets. In addition the following technologies can be researched from debris from the first contract:

  • Railguns
  • Shields
  • Plasteel Materials
  • Gravitic Sensors
  • Regenerative Hull Tissue

Nominate System for CFL Tournament

Nominate System for CFL Tournament is a contract that can only be issued after asking the Champion's Forge Live! to host a venue. Unlike other contracts Nominate System for CFL Tournament can be issued by both settled and nomadic empires. For settled empires the targeted system must be owned, while for nomadic empires the targeted system must have a waystation.

Once the contract is issued the Champion's Forge Live! arkship will move to the system and the tournament will take place in the system, allowing its environmental effects to affect all participating fleets. Only one empire can issue this contract every 20 years.

The Eternal Chorus

The Eternal Chorus is a special contract that has a 2% chance to appear every 3-5 years. The contract requires traveling to a Black Hole system and finishing a special project that needs an arkship and 90 days. After entering the system a Crystal Lattice will appear inside.

Completing the contract gives a choice between 100-1000 Trade, 350-100000 Society Research and giving a scientist 200 experience and the Erudition trait or gaining 150-100000 Unity.

After the contract is completed, the next time an arkship enters a Black Hole system the empire will gain 350-100000 Society Research and 150-100000 Unity.
