Subspace Triangulation is an event chain that will happen to every empire with the Red Giant origin. The event chain can start as early as 4 months if the approach of the Red Giant Expansion situation is Reassure the Public or as late as 20 months if the approach is Find the Cause. Finishing the event chain replaces the approaches of the Red Giant Expansion situation.

Stellar Tunneling
The truth about [capital system name] is stranger than we imagined. Ongoing investigations have revealed a twinned pair of subspace tunnels deep within our star.

One of these channels hydrogen-rich material directly into the outer shell, accelerating fusion and increasing helium production. This should drive core compression, but the second tunnel actively siphons energy, counteracting the process. The result is the expansion of the star.

Initial telemetry from both subspace tunnels suggests they connect to multiple endpoints scattered across the galaxy. Our survival depends on investigating these as soon as possible.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Red Giant origin
 Mean time to happen:

12 months

We'll need to investigate three of these locations.
  • Approaches in the Red Giant Expansion situation are replaced with Suppress Unrest and Disrupt Subspace Tunnels
  • Creates 8 special projects on gas giants 2-8 hyperlanes away from the empire capital and tracks them in the situation log

Subspace Scarring
Astrometric scans around [special project gas giant] revealed unexplained subspace scarring. While there is currently no exit point at this location, a past tunnel may have opened in orbit around this gas giant. The data has provided physics insights that could be applied in other areas.

In the meantime, we will have to look elsewhere to determine the source of the subspace tunnels at work within our star.

Alien Scrap Metal
The scans around [special project gas giant] indicate subspace scarring. We have also identified pockets of ancient scrap metal near the planet. These are orbitally locked into position, and may be related to the scarring.

Ongoing scans of these subspace fluctuations should help us to optimize the solar arrays around our home star.

While this data has been helpful, we need at least one more survey location to lock down the present source of our problems.

A Mysterious Station
Upon entering the system, our vessels encountered limited resistance from a group of automated drones. These were swiftly neutralized, allowing for a closer examination of the alien station.

The structure remains operational, though its hull bears the marks of extensive patchwork repairs. Attempting to access one of its docking bays, we made contact with a previously unknown alien species. Though linguistic barriers remain, we have confirmed that this installation is the origin of the tunnels affecting [capital system name].

Efforts are now underway to establish communications. A diplomatic resolution may be possible. Failing that, we may be forced to consider more direct methods, boarding the facility or destroying it outright. However, without a thorough understanding of its purpose, such actions may carry significant risk to our star.

Alien Negotiations
After a lengthy and difficult process, we have a rudimentary translator that can communicate with station inhabitants.

The station has been operating for generations, moving from gas giant to gas giant, using the subspace siphon to extract energy from distant stars to power their life support and powerful jump drive.

The aliens do not fully understand the systems they are operating and did not realize that their siphoning targeted stars and eventually destroyed them.

Despite their confusion, they seem eager to leave the station. They have promised to teach us what they know in exchange for a safe place to live and raise their young.

Aliens Terminated
The subspace mining station has been destroyed, killing everyone on board.

The destruction of the station detonated some unknown energy storage medium. The resulting energy rushed through the collapsing subspace tunnel into [capital system name], substantially worsening the situation back home.

That said, the transfer of hydrogen into our star has ceased. As bad as things may be, they will no longer worsen. While we may never know the origins or design of this station, we can at least rest easy knowing that such power will never fall to our rivals.

Unfortunately, we have no immediate way to return [capital system name] to its original state. If we want to affect the star's state, we'll need to rely on our recordings to piece together what the station was doing.

In a fit of remorse and before we truly grasped what they were doing, they began to dismantle the station, permanently destroying most of its components.

As a result, it will be difficult to control the star's expansion. And we must decide what to do with these volatile aliens, and whether to offer them the forgiveness they desire.

Subspace Mining Operation
The station siphons hydrogen from a gas giant and channels it through subspace into our star's shell. The resulting helium fusion releases excess energy, which is converted into Crystallized Entropy to power the station.

The inhabitants of the station retain only a warped oral tradition about its operations. They lost contact with their home civilization thousands of years ago, perhaps because that civilization collapsed or was destroyed. A buildup of calibration errors and equipment degradation has led to the increasingly unstable siphoning that now threatens to destroy [capital system name].

Working with their ancient technology we can reverse the process, but it will not be easy. Alternatively, forcing our star to go nova could expose countless deposits, transforming our heritage into a trove of exploitable resources.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Alien Negotiations or Boarding Party
  • Did not destroy the station in Lusus
 Mean time to happen:

30 days

It's time to get to work.
  • The Red Giant Expansion situation no longer progresses
  • The Red Giant Expansion situation approaches are replaced by the following: Continue Siphoning, Stabilize the Star, Cease Activity, Reverse Inflation

Prospect of Stability
After great effort, we now hold full control over the fate of [capital system name]. Left alone, it would follow its natural life cycle at its current size.

But the ancient and failing station cannot withstand repeated reconfigurations. Each adjustment strains the facility to its limits. Now, we have only one choice.

We can shrink the star back to its former size, or force it to expand, transforming the system into a vast field of easily harvested resources.

Our scientists will attempt one last reconfiguration. After that, the only way forward will be rapid disassembly, by external force.
