Star Trek New Civilisations/Traditions

Traditions tab

(ST:NC Traditions - verified for STNC-2306j)
Traditions in Star Trek: New Civilizations are similar to those of the base game; they represent the socio-cultural developments of a given empire, are unlocked using Unity, and grant various modifiers and abilities when chosen. The number of available traditions is drastically expanded compared to the base game, although the number of selectable traditions remains the same. As a result, traditions play a much greater role in defining the strengths, weaknesses and abilities of any individual empire than in the base game.

Traditions are grouped into sets known as Tradition Trees; these sets provide bonuses upon selection and when all the traditions within the set have been unlocked, along with an additional completion bonus in the form of an Ascension Perk slot. Up to seven tradition trees can be selected in a game.

The cost of an individual tradition is calculated using the same formula as in the base game with slight numerical tweaks, resulting in the following formula:

tradition cost = [ 1000 + ( 8 ⋅ num traditions ) 1.8 ] ⋅ [ 1 + ( empire size − 100 ) ⋅ 0.002 ⋅ ( 1 − empire size effect ) ] ⋅ tech/tradition cost {\displaystyle {\text{tradition cost}}=\left[1000+\left(8\cdot {\text{num traditions}}\right)^{1.8}\right]\cdot \left[1+({\text{empire size}}-100)\cdot {\text{0.002}}\cdot \left(1-{\text{empire size effect}}\right)\right]\cdot {\text{tech/tradition cost}}}

Standard Tradition Trees

These tradition trees are available to all empires except when otherwise noted. The exceptions to this are the Borg Collective, the Borg Temporal Incursion and the Undine Vanguard, who are barred from selecting these trees outside of a few specific circumstances.

The Acquisition tradition tree focuses on Trading Stations and loss-cutting tactics. It is available to all regular empires.
