Star Trek New Civilisations/Game settings

Game modification: Star Trek New Civilisations

The following game settings may be chosen at the start of a new playthrough. The majority of settings are identical to those found in vanilla Stellaris.

Setting Effects Options
Galaxy Setup The map for the playthrough, based on canon Star Trek locations.
  • Milky Way Galaxy: Small (700 systems)
  • Milky Way Galaxy: Medium (1000+ systems)
  • Milky Way Galaxy: Large (1400+ systems)
  • Alpha/Beta Quadrant: Small (600 systems)
  • Alpha/Beta Quadrant: Large (900 systems)
  • Gamma Quadrant: Small (600 systems)
  • Delta Quadrant: Small (600 systems)
  • Alpha Octant: Small (500 systems)
  • Birth of the Federation: Small (400 systems)
Prime Universe Whether factions and events follow the Prime Universe or Mirror Universe.
  • On - Factions and events follow the Prime Universe
  • Off - Factions and events follow the Mirror Universe
AI Empires The number of AI adversaries. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • (number) - dependent on map size
Tech/Tradition Cost Scales the cost of Tech, Traditions, and Ambitions. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • (multiplier) - from 0.25x to 5x
Habitable Worlds The chance of a planet in the goldilocks zone of a star being habitable. Due to the large number of predefined systems based on Star Trek canon, you will generally find more habitable worlds in STNC than vanilla Stellaris, regardless of this setting.
  • (multiplier) - from 0.25x to 5x
Free Warp Whether all nearby systems are always connected by warp lanes.
  • On - all neighboring systems are connected
  • Off - system connections are generated randomly as in vanilla Stellaris
Warp Lane Density Scales the number of Warp Lanes generated. Identical to vanilla Stellaris, but ignored if Free Warp is On.
  • (multiplier) - from 0.5x to 2.75x
  • Full - removes clusters and choke points
Mid-Game Start Year Earliest year that mid-game events occur. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • (year)
End-Game Start Year Earliest year that end-game events occur. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • (year)
Victory Year Year at which the empire with the highest score wins. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • (year)
  • Off - the game does not end at a predefined date
Pre-Warp Civilisations Scales the number of pre-warp and pre-sapient civilisations created at game start. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • Off - no pre-warp or pre-sapient civilisations created
  • (number) - from 0.25x to 5x
Difficulty Scales the bonuses provided to the player (below Ensign) or AI (above Ensign). Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • Civilian
  • Cadet
  • Ensign
  • Captain
  • 'Commodore
  • Admiral
  • Grand Admiral
Scaling Difficulty Difficulty bonuses scale from 0 at the beginning of the game to their full weight at a defined point. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • Off - difficulty bonuses are active from the start of the game
  • Mid-Game - difficulty bonuses scale to their full level at the Mid-Game start year
  • End-Game - difficulty bonuses scale to their full level at the End-Game start year
AI Aggressiveness How likely the AI is to declare wars and use fleets offensively. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • Low
  • Normal
  • High
Difficulty Adjusted AI Modifiers Economic bonuses from Tech are multiplied by AI difficulty bonuses. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • On
  • Off
Logistic Growth Ceiling Scales pop growth bonuses when planets are in the middle of their growth curve. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • (multiplier) - from 1x to 2x
Growth Scaling Scales the required growth to create a new pop. Identical to vanilla Stellaris.
  • (multiplier) - from 0x to 1x
Canonical Bypasses Whether FTL bypasses such as the Bajoran Wormhole and Underspace appear in the game.
  • On - FTL bypasses appear on maps that include them
  • Off - FTL bypasses do not appear
Ironman Mode Disables console cheats and manual saves. Identical to vanilla Stellaris. Note that Achievements are not possible when playing STNC, regardless of this setting.
  • On
  • Off
Minor Empires Scales the number of empires that start the game with Domestic Focus, and will not expand from their home system.
  • (multiplier) - from 0x to 5x
