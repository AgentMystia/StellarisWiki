Torpedoes

Many starships and space stations are equipped with powerful guided torpedoes of one form or another.

Name Slots Effects Description
Spatial Torpedo
  • 15% shield penetration
  • 25% armor penetration
 While primitive, spatial torpedoes are able to inflict significant damage on ship hulls unprotected by energy shielding.
Photon Torpedo
  • 25% shield penetration
  • 25% armor penetration

Photon Torpedoes are warp-capable tactical matter/antimatter weapons commonly deployed aboard starships and starbases. Damage is conveyed primarily by the creation of ultra-high energy photons following the annihilation of the matter/antimatter charge.

Quantum Torpedo
  • 25% shield penetration
  • 25% armor penetration
 Quantum torpedo warheads rely on rapid energy extraction from zero-point vacuum. The detonation of a photon torpedo warhead, enriched with fluoronetic vapor, powers a space-time continuum distortion emitter.
Plasma Torpedo
  • 25% shield penetration
  • 35% armor penetration
  • 80% damage against shield
  • 110% damage against hull
  • Additional damage scaling with target size
 Employing high energy plasma confined in a magnetic envelope, plasma torpedoes can devastate unprotected hulls.
Polaron Torpedo
  • 45% shield penetration
  • 25% armor penetration
  • 90% damage against armor
  • 90% damage against hull
 Polaron torpedoes are able to bypass layers of shielding before reaching their target's hull.
Gravimetric Torpedo
  • +6% tracking
  • 50% armor penetration
  • 125% damage against shield
 A gravimetric torpedo can be created by outfitting a standard photon torpedo with a gravimetric charge. The resulting gravimetric sheer effect overloads shields and can cause severe hull damage, even in heavily armoured targets.
Magnetometric Torpedo
  • −10% accuracy
  • +6% tracking
  • 200% damage against shield
  • 75% damage against armor
  • 50% damage against hull
 Magnetometric Torpedoes are capable of causing substantial damage to enemy shields. Power requirements are somewhat higher than other torpedoes, while damage against hull and armour is reduced.
Tricobalt Torpedo
  • 25% shield penetration
  • 35% armor penetration
  • 125% damage against shield
  • 110% damage against armor
  • Additional damage scaling with target size
 Tricobalt Torpedoes utilise the titular heavy isotope to create a thermokinetic explosion capable of extending deep into subspace.
Resonance Torpedo
  • 35% shield penetration
  • 35% armor penetration
  • 125% damage against shield
  • 75% damage against armor
 Using exotic matter to trigger wildly oscillating quantum resonance charge-coupling effects, Resonance Torpedoes are effective against shielding but less so against reinforced armour plating.
Temporal Torpedo
  • 35% shield penetration
  • 50% armor penetration
  • 90% damage against shield
  • 95% damage against armor
 Temporal, typically chroniton-based, torpedoes sacrifice warhead yield in favour of an onboard temporal field generator. They are capable of passing directly through a vessels shields and armour, by maintaining a constant state of temporal flux.
Thermionic Torpedo
  • 25% shield penetration
  • 25% armor penetration
  • 80% damage against shield
  • 150% damage against armor
  • 125% damage against hull
  • Additional damage scaling with target size
 Thermionic torpedoes generate waves of thermionic radiation upon detonation. This radiation is extremely harmful to organic lifeforms, but dissipates relatively quickly.
Positron Torpedo
  • 25% shield penetration
  • 25% armor penetration
 Positron torpedoes are larger and slower than most contemporary torpedoes but have extremely high destructive potential. Each torpedo detonation generates a massive burst of positrons, which annihilate with electrons on contact.
Merculite Missiles
  • 15% shield penetration
  • 25% armor penetration
  • Spread Fire (ability to target more than one target simultaneously)
 While relatively primitive compared to other torpedo or missile weapon systems, Merculite Missiles are cheap to produce and require little maintenance. In large enough volleys, they inflict crippling damage.
Tetryon Torpedo
  • 20% shield penetration
  • 20% armor penetration
  • 120% damage against shield
  • 95% damage against armor
 Tetryon torpedoes release highly unstable subatomic tetryon particles, which can normally only exist in subspace and decay energetically in normal space. The resulting radiation depletes energy shields and erodes unshielded matter, but is ineffective against heavy armour.
Transphasic Torpedo
  • 35% shield penetration
  • 35% armor penetration
  • 110% damage against shield
  • 110% damage against armor
  • Additional on-hit effects
 Transphasic torpedoes generate a destructive subspace compression pulse that reacts constructively with transwarp drive core resonances to amplify damage further.

Hull Plating

Hull plating is the external armour component of a space station or vessel's hull which provides resistance against space debris and enemy weapons fire.

Name Effects Description
Minimal Hull Plating
  • No effects
  • No cost
Hull Plating
  • Provides 100% armor
  • Provides 100% hull
 New materials and reinforcement techniques allow for more robust and resilient starship frames.
Heavy Hull Plating
  • Provides increased armor scaling with ship size
  • Provides 110% hull
  • −5% Evasion
 New materials and reinforcement techniques allow for significantly more robust and resilient starship frames at the expense of ship manoeuvrability.
Stealth Composite
  • Provides armor scaling with ship size
  • Provides 100% hull
  • Increased evasion chance (multiplicative)
  • Increased Alpha-Strike Duration after Decloaking
 While not the most durable of materials, stealth composites are abe to reduce a vessel's detectability considerably.
Crystal Lattice
  • Provides 90% hull
  • Increases shield capacity
  • Increases shield regeneration
  • Increased Shield Reduction Duration after Decloaking
 Nano-weave crystal lattice frameworks can magnify resonant frequencies used by most forms of energy shielding.
Organic Hull Carapace
  • Provides additional armor
  • Provides armor regeneration
  • Requires food & alloys
 Engineered biological carapaces are capable of absorbing and recovering from significant damage.
Regenerative Hull Plating
  • Provides additional armor
  • Provides armor regeneration
  • Provides additional hull
  • Provides hull regeneration
 Swarms of repair-drones coupled with redundant and modular designs allow for rapid recovery of hull and armour integrity.

Shields

Many starships and space stations are equipped with powerful shielding of one form or another.

Name Effects Description
Minimal Armour Plating
  • No effects
  • No cost
 Without bulky armour or energy shielding the cost of producing a vessel can be reduced.
Polarised Hull Plating
  • Provides additional armor
 Polarised Hull Plating exploits nano-scale electro-weak force coupling effects to harden a material beyond normal limits.
Deflector Shields
  • Provides 100% shield capacity
  • Provides 100% shield regeneration
 Deflector Shields utilise layers of energetic quantum distortion waves to mitigate damage to anything within the shield bubble.
Covariant Shields
  • Provides 110% shield capacity
  • Provides 90% shield regeneration
 Covariant shielding extends the frequencies used to generate the deflection field, effectively increasing their capacity at the cost of a slower rate of regeneration.
Regenerative Shields
  • Provides 90% shield capacity
  • Provides 110% shield regeneration
 Regenerative shielding utilises an array of secondary and tertiary generators in order to increase the apparent regeneration rate of a vessel's shielding.

This comes at the expense of a reduced peak damage capacity.

Resilient Shields
  • Provides 95% shield capacity
  • Provides 95% shield regeneration
  • Provides additional shield hardening
 Resilient shielding utilises many of the principles behind hull polarisation in order to create a deflection field capable of directly reinforcing the armour of the vessel.
Refractive Shields
  • Provides 95% shield capacity
  • Provides 95% shield regeneration
  • Improves cloaking abilities for fleets that can cloak
  • 120% power usage
 Refractive shielding magnifies the effect of cloaking devices, providing greatly enhanced stealth and ambush capabilities at the cost of increased power consumption.
Bio-Electric Field
  • Provides additional hull
  • Provides hull regeneration
  • Requires food & components
 Bio-Electric Fields use organic processes to form an electro-dynamic layer surrounding a vessel, reinforcing its integrity.
Subspace Field Generator
  • Provides additional armor
  • Provides armor regeneration
 Subspace electromagnetic fields provide considerable defensive capacity. While vulnerable to high damage over a short period, the ability to quickly regenerate field strength affords long term survivability.
