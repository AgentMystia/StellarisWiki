This article is for the PC version of Stellaris only.

Precursors are long dead galactic empires predating even the fallen empires and the curators. Their ruins are scattered across the galaxy for the player to find. Only one precursor event chain (out of 5 available) is placed in a campaign during the galaxy generation.

Upon starting a precursor event chain the player has to successfully survey 6 precursor related anomalies (to gather their artifacts) in order to find the location of the precursor's home system. The first empire to survey said home world receives large amounts of resources as a reward. If a player has ran out of planets to survey (therefore no possible anomalies) a precursor anomaly will be generated every ~30 years.

This is a partial list of events relating to precursors (ids 1500 – 2300 from Stellaris/events/precursor_events.txt).

The Irassian Concordat
Please help with verifying or updating this infobox. It was last verified for version 3.11.
We have recovered artifacts from an ancient alien civilization on [planet name]! From what we have translated so far of their language, we have learned that these aliens called themselves the "Irassian Concordat." They were an interstellar power that held sway over this region of the galaxy a little over a million years ago.

They appear to have been six-limbed mammalians, and there are several references to some sort of plague called the "Javorian Pox" which swept across their empire with devastating results, possibly leading to their extinction.

The Irassian Homeworld
Please help with verifying or updating this infobox. It was last verified for version 3.0.
Irassia – the former homeworld of the Irassian Concordat. Someone has relatively recently (within the last 2,000 years) gone through the trouble of systematically bombarding every square meter of the [FromFrom.GetPlanetMoon]'s surface from orbit – possibly a misguided attempt to keep the Javorian Pox from resurfacing.

Despite this, the surface is still covered in the fossilized remains of the billions of Irassians who succumbed to the plague in the final years of their empire. By the time it had spread to their homeworld, most of the worlds in the Irassian Concordat had already fallen, and their alien vassals were in open revolt.

Judging by ancient satellite logs recovered from orbit, the mood among the population on the Irassian homeworld was largely resigned by this point. The widespread panic that accompanied the first outbreaks of the pox on the colonies was largely absent, as most had come to accept the fact that the end was drawing near.
