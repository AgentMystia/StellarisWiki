Policies

Version

This article has been verified for the current PC version (4.4) of the game.
Policies window

Policies outline the stance of an empire on other empires, treatment of pops, and acceptable behavior. Once altered, a specific policy cannot be changed until 10 years have passed. Some resolutions in the Galactic Community can make certain policies a breach of galactic law. In this case the policy will show a warning sign next to it.

Changing policies is advised as a way to mitigate faction unhappiness in large empires that have a lot of pops of different ethics, besides the possibility of opening up new possible avenues of power.

Orbital Surrender Acceptance

Orbital Surrender Acceptance determines whether surrenders caused by orbital bombardment are accepted. AI empires will always accept surrenders. This policy is not available to Genocidal empires as planets will never surrender to them. Bombarding a world for the first time will bring the option to set the initial policy, which cannot be changed for at least 10 years.

Type Effects Description
Prohibited Surrenders are not accepted
There will be no surrender. Orbital Bombardment will continue indefinitely.
Allowed Surrenders are accepted
Foreign citizens have the right to surrender.

Trade Policy

Trade Policy determines what resources Trade be converted into. The policy is renamed to Logistics Policy for Gestalt Consciousness empires. AI always uses Holy Covenant, Mutual Aid or Trade League if it can.

Trade Policy adds an amount of each resource equal to the Trade generated from internal empire activity (jobs and pops), multiplied by Exchange Rate. For example, if jobs and pops each produce 50 Trade value, for a total of 100 Trade, a Trade Policy with 25% Exchange Rate for Consumer Goods will add 25 Consumer Goods to production. Trade Policy can affect Trade itself; a 100% Exchange Rate for the Trade resource effectively doubles your Trade production.

Trade income from Commercial Pacts and Branch Offices are not included in Trade Policy calculations. Gains or losses from Monthly Trades do not affect Trade Policy calculations.

Nomad Border Policy policy is available to settled empires and determines what happens when ships belonging to a Nomadic empire enter their borders. The penalty is avoided if the ship's Cloaking Strength is higher than the starbase's Detection Strength.
