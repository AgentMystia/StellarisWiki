Star Wars New Dawn/Game settings

Game settings are the configurable options a player can choose before creating a new game.

Galaxy

Selection of galaxies divided into two types: Lore-Rich Static Galaxy or Freeplay Static Galaxy. Sizes range from 300+ star systems to 2200+. Empire quantity ranges from 28 to 42 stellar empires.

Lore-Rich Galaxy

Canon Star Wars galaxy within one map. Empires' starting territory spans multiple systems.

Freeplay Galaxy

More traditional Stellaris start within the Star Wars galaxy. Each empire has one starting system and planet.

Galaxy Size

Four different galaxy size options are available for both Lore-Rich and Freeplay galaxies.

Game Details

Galactic New Order - If selected, the player or AI that is playing the Galactic Empire (faction) will become Galactic Emperor (feature/mechanic) once the Galactic Community is established. The New Order Ascension Perk also becomes available to that empire. The perk activates the crisis mechanic and depending on crisis level can lead to the outbreak of the Rebellion.

Habitable Worlds - Increases / decreases the chance of planets being habitable. Selectable between 0x-5x (values over 1x normalised to 1x).

Technology and Tradition Cost - Determines how quickly Technology and Traditions are acquired.

Mid-Game Start Year - Determines earliest year that Mid-Game Crises can trigger. Default: 2300

End-Game Star Year - Determines earliest year that End-Game Crises can trigger. Default: 2400

Victory Year - Determines at what year the game can end. The empire with the highest score is declared winner. Default: 2500

Difficulty - Determines positive modifiers for player and AI dependent on selection.

Scaling Difficulty - Causes the player or AI empires difficulty bonuses to start at zero and scale up over the course of the game, reaching the bonus defined by difficulty level at the Mid-Game or End-Game year.

Difficulty Adjusted AI Modifiers - Multiplies empire-wide economic modifiers for AI empires on harder difficulties.

AI Aggressiveness - Determines how likely the AI is to declare war on empires if they have negative attitude, as well as how many fleets will it use for offence.

Logistic Growth Ceiling - Determines the pop growth bonuses when planets are in the middle of their logistic curve. Higher values negatively affect late-game performance.

Growth Required Scaling - Determines how many growth points are required for new pops to be created. Lower values negatively affect late-game performance.

Ironman Mode - Game mode that restricts player's ability to control their own save file, effectively removing the ability to correct mistakes or retroactively change decisions. Manual save is disabled and game runs on a single save file which is overwritten at regular intervals and when a significant action is taken. Console commands also disabled.

Crisis Strength - Determines the bonus that End-Game Crisis ships receive. Ranges from 0x (no crisis) to 25x. Base crisis ship bonus is multiplied by the number of this slider.

Trade Relays - Links systems along major hyperspace routes with relay megastructures placed near the centre solar entity. Interstellar travel along these routes is significantly faster.

Difficulty

Determines difficulty of a game by granting bonuses to player or AI empires. Bonuses may affect resource acquisition, naval capacity, stability, trade value, weapons damage, hull/armour/shield strength, resettlement cost, and more.
