The Bickering Commanders is an empire event chain that can happen if the empire is a Bulwark specialized subject. The event chain can have significant consequences on the empire.

The Bickering Commanders
We have seen a rise in disagreements and arguing between some of our commanders and leaders. If [commander 1] thinks we should go through with something, you can be sure that [commander 2] will argue that we should do the opposite.

At first it seemed to only be a good-natured rivalry between equals, but lately it has become ever more toxic and hostile, and we are starting to see their peers taking sides as well.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Democratic or Oligarchic
  • Not Pacifist
  • At least two Commanders
 Mean time to happen:

5 years

We will end this bickering, one way or another.
They will figure it out, in time.

A Split Admiralty
No longer is it a simple case of disagreeing, as the whole admiralty have reached a point of outright hostility to one another. What at first were two commanders arguing has now split the whole office in two, and there is no longer anyone trying to broker peace between them.

In addition to splitting the admiralty, they have now also split into two clearly defined political groups, one who believes the admiralty should play a strong role in our government, and one that thinks the general populace should be given more political power.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • The Bickering Commanders
  • 50% chance
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

How long will they keep this nonsense going?

Overlord Intervened
As we feared the conflicts in the admiralty would spread to the rest of the leadership of our empire, our ally [overlord name] stepped in and managed to calm things. It seems not even [comamnder 1] or [commander 2] are willing to oppose the leadership in [overlord capital name].
 
Trigger conditions:
  • A Split Admiralty
  • 75% chance
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

Finally, an end to the arguments.

Overlord Disruption
We had high hopes that the problems between [commander 1] and [commander 2] would finally come to an end, when diplomats from [overlord name] arrived to help broker peace in the conflict. But after days of continuous bickering, it seems the only thing the two sides have agreed upon is that our overlord has no part to play in their disputes.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • A Split Admiralty
  • 25% chance
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

Not even they could help?

Murderous Intent
It has been weeks and months of the conflict between the two sides of the admiralty, one led by [commander 1] and the other led by [commander 2], and yet there has not been any outright violence. Until now - with the discovery of the body of one of the democratic supporters.

Though we have found no proof that the other side is involved in the suspected murder, this is sure to spark even harsher and more violent responses from the two sides moving forward.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Overlord Disruption
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

What can be done to salvage this situation?

A Question of Funding
Now no longer contained simply to the admiralty, we see the arguments and conflicts in every office across our empire. The latest incident involves funds earmarked for civil needs on [capital name], which the oligarchs want to re-allocate for the military.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Murderous Intent
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

It was earmarked for a reason.
  • Democratic choice
  • Civil Funding modifier added for 20 years, giving the following effects:
    • Amenities +10
    • Planetary Build Speed +15%
The oligarchs are right, move the funding to the military.
  • Oligarchic choice
  • Military Funding modifier added for 10 years, giving the following effects:
    • Ship Fire Rate +5%
    • Ship Upkeep −5%

A Public Disaster
A report has come back to us, that an unidentified cargo ship has crashed into a residential area on [random colony]. All who used to live there have temporarily been moved into shelters, while we are seeing what we can do to help.

The oligarchic group has advocated for not only ignoring the plight of those evicted from their homes, but to open up a new mineral mine in the district, as it seems the cargo ship found a whole rich vein that we have not seen before now. Their rivals, of course, deeply oppose the idea, and demand that we focus our efforts on rebuilding the homes of those affected.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Murderous Intent
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

And rebuild their homes we shall.
  • Democratic choice
Minerals, you say?
  • Oligarchic choice
  • Minerals Gained: 500
  • Adds the Crashed Cargo Ship planetary feature to the colony

Violence in the Streets
After a particularly violent protest close to the offices of the oligarchic faction, several members of both factions have been brought into question. As expected, both sides have incredibly different stories, mostly blaming their rivals for everything that went down.

Not only do they want us to choose a side, but both sides are willing to step in and make sure that it is worth our time.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Murderous Intent
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

The populists have the right of it.
  • Democratic choice
  • Influence Gained: 40
Clearly, the oligarchs were innocent in this attack.
  • Oligarchic choice
  • Energy Credits Gained: 100-1000

The Brewing Storm
We have seen the conflict play out not only in the admiralty itself, but all across our empire as well. Though most of the violence have been kept out of the public eye, both sides have seen significant losses at this point.

Our informants on both sides have informed us that both are now eyeing the leadership of the nation itself, and are planning to take it by force so they can get rid of their opponents. It seems at this point even our [ruler title] will have to take a side, or else face removal by the other.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • A Question of Funding
  • A Public Disaster
  • Violence in the Streets
 Mean time to happen:

5 months

The populist movement of [commander 1].
  • Democratic choice
The military leadership of [commander 2].
  • Oligarchic choice

The End of the Rivalry
After the conflict came to a head in the admiralty itself, it seems the democratic faction has beaten back their opponents, as they take control of many of our central faculties.

With everything under their control, the democratic commanders bring their old rivals through a short mock prosecution.

Shortly after getting rid of their opponents, they decide that they will impose their own government policies on our empire.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • The Brewing Storm
  • 50% chance, doubled for every democratic choice
 Mean time to happen:

15 days

Let us hope the worst is over with.
  • A Commander is killed
  • 63% chance authority changes to Democratic if not already and ethics shift to Egalitarian if Authoritarian
  • 20% chance the ruler is replaced by a Commander

The End of the Rivalry
After the conflict came to a head in the admiralty itself, it seems the oligarchic faction has taken the victory in the brewing conflict, as a widespread announcement is shared across all our colonies.

After making their public announcement, it is followed by a speech denouncing their old rivals.

Shortly after getting rid of their opponents, they decide that they will impose their own government policies on our empire.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • The Brewing Storm
  • 50% chance, doubled for every oligarchic choice
 Mean time to happen:

15 days

Let us hope the worst is over with.
  • A Commander is killed
  • 63% chance authority changes to Oligarchic if not already and ethics shift to Authoritarian if Egalitarian
  • 20% chance the ruler is replaced by a Commander
