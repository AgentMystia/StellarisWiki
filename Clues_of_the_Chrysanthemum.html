Clues of the Chrysanthemum is a very short and linear event chain available only to the Gundersen Research Society preset empire. It will start within a few months after the game starts and is entirely optional. It is mostly narrative, with only the final event having a minor reward.

Contents

  • 1 A Voice from the Past
  • 2 Signal Found
  • 3 Unity

A Voice from the Past
We have found no traces of the four ark ships that passed through the wormhole before the Chrysanthemum arrived at Unity. One ship, the Hyacinth, was scheduled to make the passage after us, and the Chrysanthemum's long-range sensors reported a brief energy spike from the wormhole shortly before it destabilized and vanished.

Assuming the Hyacinth survived the passage and made it to our galactic neighborhood, the ark ship has so far failed to establish contact with us. It might be a good idea to search nearby systems for signs of its presence.
 
Trigger conditions:

Playing as the Gundersen Research Society preset empire

Signal Found
A Ulysses Initiative relay buoy is drifting in this system - battered, barely functional, but still broadcasting.

Beyond the original announcement, the buoy carries two later transmissions. The second is dated roughly ten years after the first.

Colony population: two hundred and sixty thousand. Crops established. Infrastructure growing.

The third transmission is dated from Day 9,182 - approximately twenty-five years after landing. "We are still here. Unity is more challenging than initial surveys indicated. We have lost people. If any Ulysses Initiative vessel receives this broadcast, please respond. We are here. We endure."

Unity is in the Deneb system, only a few hyperlane jumps from here.

Unity
The Deneb system is quiet.

The Hyacinth drops from transit to find no beacons, no signals, no ships moving between worlds. Olympus hangs in the middle distance, enormous and indifferent.

One moon is wrong. Where the colony's broadcasts described green continents and open water, the scanners show a dead world - the unmistakable gray-brown of a nuked planet. Nothing lives there.

And in low orbit, drifting slow and dark, is the Chrysanthemum. Not destroyed. Simply empty. Every porthole dark. The ship that carried a quarter million colonists to this world has sat here for decades.
