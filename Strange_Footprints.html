Strange Footprints is a colony event chain that has a very small chance to trigger 2 years after a colony has been founded on a Continental, Savannah or Tropical planet. It can only happen once per game.

Strange Footprints
Reports have come in from our outlying agricultural stations on [colony name]. Several Farmers have vanished without a trace, their homesteads eerily undisturbed, save for the appearance of immense footprints in the surrounding soil.

The tracks are unlike anything examined in the planetary survey, quadrupedal, broad-toed with claws, and deep enough to suggest a creature of considerable mass. The colonists worry that we may be dealing with some unknown predator.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Not capital world
  • Not Xenophobe
  • Not Gestalt Consciousness
  • Not Fanatic Purifiers or Scorched World Heralds
  • Not Mechanical or Machine founder species
 Mean time to happen:

24 months

A Dead Monster
The expedition into the wilderness proved taxing, but ultimately fruitful. After days of following the creature's trail, our team arrived at a site of devastation: the corpse of the beast displays a heavily chitinous exoskeleton, thorned spinal ridges, and a segmented tail ending in a single curved stinger. The body is perforated by multiple punctures.

Beyond it, concealed beneath a highly effective cloaking canopy of bioluminescent vines, our instruments detect an ancient garden. Marble columns entwined with ivy, smooth obsidian paths. Our team approached carefully until we caught a glimpse of the garden's caretakers: a humanoid species, elegant in bearing, their skin subtly luminous. Despite the harmonious appearance, the flowers and vines offered an effective veil. We would need to penetrate this glinting cover in order to approach the ones who lived beyond.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Strange Footprints
  • Paid Energy
 Mean time to happen:

The colonists are no longer merely unsettled; they are terrified. Several families have already abandoned their holdings for the colony center.

All Life Must End
A colossal entity emerged from the shadows, neither beast nor machine, but something stranger, its form shifting between molten geometry and seething shadow. It did not attempt to communicate. It simply devoured.

Buildings crumbled into the ground. Colonists were torn apart, vanishing into the air or crushed under invisible pressure. Within hours, nothing remained but a low hum echoing across the ruined plain.

Then, as soon as it came, the beast vanished, leaving behind a trail of death and devastation on a scorched planet.

A Swift Attack
There was no warning. King Tamperion emerged from the heart of the gardens, draped in ceremonial armor spun from living vines and bioluminescent blossoms. With each deliberate step along the colony's edge, the world itself awakened. Vines slithered from the soil, while petals unfurled like blades. The air grew thick with a golden pollen, fine and luminescent. It seeped into vents and lungs. Within hours, it had claimed the life of many, inducing seizures, madness, and finally, death.

Only the intervention of automated colony defenses eventually brought him down. King Tamperion fell without a word; witnesses say his final gaze held not hatred, but a deep and solemn disappointment.

Where the Court of Blossoms once was, our scouts found only forest. It is as though the kingdom had folded itself back into the soil, taking its secrets and its sorrow with it.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • A Dead Monster
  • Chose to establish a colony
 Mean time to happen:

A Seedling of Doubt
What began as a peaceful exchange has taken on a subtler, more troubling dimensions. Participation in Anathari rites has resulted in delayed reports, fragmented memories, and altered sleep. King Tamperion remains courteous, but increasingly evasive when asked about the deeper purpose of these rites. His explanations, though poetic, often deflect direct inquiry. Even his title, 'Goldenhearted', appears in no recorded Anathari doctrine. Some now suggest the rituals serve not just as cultural bonding, but as tools of psychological calibration.

Whatever the method, the result is unmistakable: we are becoming aligned with Tamperion and his kin. When he speaks of the Dark Immortal, it feels less like foreign entreaty and more like a sacred duty to help our dearest allies, the Anathari.

The Dark Immortal
The scientists deciphered the essence successfully and located the Dark Immortal at a forlorn mountain top. Soon after, an expedition team arrived at the mountain's plateau, finding the site occupied.

At the epicenter loomed the architect of the trials: The Dark Immortal. An entity of incomprehensible origin, its form an unstable fusion of plasma, smoke, bone, and psionically active matter. Its voice bypassed sound, manifesting directly in the neural architecture of every team member present.

'Have you come to subordinate yourselves, little [founder species insult]?' it intoned slowly, its thought-voice laced with mockery. 'The Anathari are my dearest possession. But I offer you a chance. Enter the trials and prove yourself worthy of my regard.'

What Lies Beyond Death
Our action was swift. A militia strike team infiltrated the Court of Blossoms. King Tamperion was meditating in the garden when we struck. But the king did not fall easily. Witnesses describe a terrifying display of botanical wrath, vines erupting from stone, floral constructs clawing through steel. By the time the king was brought down, the garden was soaked in luminous blood and ash.

What remained beneath the garden astonished us, as it was a vault of biological treasures: ancient knowledge, botanical engineering, and stores of genetic stock from countless harvest cycles. The soil, now unbound from its ancient caretakers, was more fertile than any yet found on the planet. We may be entering a golden age of abundance.

Trial of Bravery
A single colonist was transported to a cave deep within the mountain, The Dark Immortal leading the way. A monstrous beast awaited in the darkest corner. The entity resembled a crustacean of impossible scale, its shell glittering with strange minerals and ancient alloys. It acted like it was guarding its turf, moving with an eerie coordination that hinted at a surprising level of intelligence.

War is the only Answer
We knew the power of the Dark Immortal was immense. Our assault was immediate and violent. The cavern trembled as the Dark Immortal let out a churning screech.

While our forces took heavy damage, we managed to bind the Dark Immortal. The cavern howled, and the air cracked like splitting bone. Then the Dark Immortal was gone.

Afterward, King Tamperion met the survivors. He approached the colonists and bowed deeply. For all that had been lost, the nightmare was over.

Succeeding the Trial of Bravery
Steel met shell in the pitch-dark cavern as the chosen colonist charged into the abyss.

The titanic Crustacean reacted instantly, its claws slicing through stone, its bulk shaking the mountain. But the colonist was ready. Luring it into tighter corridors, they baited the creature's movements, striking at joints and sensory nodes identified in advance.

When the dust settled, the creature lay still, its final convulsion echoing through the mountain like thunder. The colonist emerged, covered in blood and dust, but alive.

The Dark Immortal gave a slow nod, as if in appreciation of the colonist's reckless actions. The trial of bravery was completed.

Failing the Trial of Bravery
The beast's claws were lightning-fast and hummed faintly in the cave's darkness, each movement a blur, each strike accompanied by a low-frequency pulse that set stone trembling.

The colonist moved carefully, considering their options. A feint, a lure, an attempted blow, or perhaps a distraction. But the creature adapted faster than expected. Its next strikes landed true, and the cavern lit up in a burst of bioluminescent rage.

When the dust cleared, the colonist lay dead. We had failed the trial.

Trial of Sacrifice
A colonist stepped forward to face the trial, and was swept up to stand on a vast, darkened dais. In the center: two obsidian chairs and a long, low table. A single weapon was placed upon it, an elegant, curved blade. As the colonist picked it up with one hand, it hummed faintly with power.

In one chair sat King Tamperion, bound not by rope, but by luminous cords of elemental force. His expression was unreadable, showing neither fear nor peace, only stillness. The other chair was empty.

Then the voice spoke into the colonist's mind once more.

'The blade has tasted kings and cowards alike,' the Dark Immortal whispered. 'To pass this trial, one life must be taken. Take the Anathari leader's heart, or sacrifice yourself; the choice is yours. Choose wrongly... and the trial will end.'

Succeeding the Trial of Sacrifice
The colonist stood before the bound form of King Tamperion, silent, unmoving. Upon the obsidian table, the blade awaited. The Dark Immortal offered no guidance.

The colonist took the blade and drove it cleanly into Tamperion's chest, swift and decisive. He made no cry. There was only a soft metallic chime, like the distant clanging of bells.

King Tamperion looked up, eyes calm. The blade, buried to the hilt, had not pierced flesh, but something else entirely. His chest glowed faintly, pulsing with a golden light. King Tamperion the 'Goldenhearted' indeed...

The bindings around the king dissolved and the dais faded. The Dark Immortal spoke once more into our minds: 'You may proceed.'

Failing the Trial of Sacrifice
The colonist looked upon King Tamperion, blade in hand. King Tamperion sat still, his soft gaze offering neither plea nor permission. The colonist hesitated, then turned the blade on themself.

A sharp intake of breath, and the colonist collapsed, the blade clattering against the obsidian floor. The choice was clear: to spare the Anathari leader and to carry the burden alone.

But the Dark Immortal's words arrived with neither anger nor triumph: 'Compassion is not absolution. The blade was never meant for the self.'

A silent tear rolled down King Tamperion's face. With the best intentions, we had sealed his people's fate. The trial ended, not in glory, but sorrow. The colonist was dead, and the path forward was sealed.

There was a pause. A silence that stretched across thought and time, but then a hushed agreement. 'The Anathari are no longer mine to watch,' the Dark Immortal spoke. 'You are bound now.' With that, it vanished. No light or sound, only a change in the air, and the sense that something vast has departed.

In the cycles that followed first contact, the interspecies convergence between colonists and the Anathari intensified. Crops flourished extensively, and cities rose in harmony with the land. Flora and technology walked side by side. For the first time in centuries, [colony name] flourished, its atmosphere no longer burdened by conflict, but recalibrated for peace.

A Rain of Golden Petals
'As you wish.'

The Dark Immortal withdrew from our thoughts, and with it, something fundamental shifted. The colonists felt a deep, unsettling churning in their spines. All across the planet, the Anathari began to collapse. No scream was reported, no struggle, only the soft rustle of robes folding into dust, like petals falling in the whisper of a breeze.

In the chamber, King Tamperion lingered a moment longer. His eyes blinked in utter betrayal and disappointment for the [founder species plural] species. He raised no hand, spoke no plea. A single golden bloom fell from his sleeve to the cavern floor. And then he, too, was gone. There was no mourning, nor relief. Only the absence of what once was, a people, a song, a civilization, swept from [colony name].

The Dark Immortal seemed pleased.
