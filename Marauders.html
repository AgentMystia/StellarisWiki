Marauders are void dwelling militaristic FTL societies that have eschewed planetary dwelling in favor of living on ships and stations and are always hostile but will not attack empires unless they enter their systems or the marauders are in the process of raiding them. A Marauder empire can be eradicated by destroying all stations and void dwellings in their home systems, but these are well defended and will take a powerful mid to late-game navy to deal with them. Each Marauder empire holds 3 systems populated by Void Dwellings, defense platforms and fleets, with the home system having very large deposits of Energy or Minerals. Up to 3 marauder empires can appear:

  • If the Apocalypse DLC is installed, the spawning or marauder empires can be customized at galaxy settings.
  • If the Nomads DLC is installed, at least one empire has the Heirs of the Khan origin, and the galaxy does not have a marauder empire, a 1-system marauder empire will be created.

If an empire with the Heirs of the Khan origin exists, one of the marauder empires will be of the same species. The origin empire will start in contact with it but cannot interact with it. They also cannot ask marauders to raid another empire or recruit leaders from them.

Dormancy

Marauder Home System

Marauders will spend the majority of each game in a dormant state, unable to expand beyond their starting systems and without any centralized authority beyond a series of barbaric warlords. Each Marauder empire is assigned one of three sets of dialogue but aside from that they function identically.

Marauder Empires refuse to interact with Genocidal empires, meaning they cannot make use of their mercenary or raiding services.

Raiding

Marauders will occasionally set out to raid settled empires they have established contact with. Before raiding, they will offer said empire a chance to pay them off with a hefty tribute. Targeted empires can choose to pay Minerals, Energy or Food, the value depending on their monthly income.

If the Marauders are not paid, they will spawn a separate raiding fleet neutral to all but the targeted empire. While the raid is ongoing, they can still be bought off with tribute, but the price is doubled. Once Marauder raiders reach an undefended planet, they will bombard it until it reaches 15 Devastation, at which point the Marauders will steal either resources or up to 4 biological Pops.

Other empires can also pay the Marauders to conduct a raid on one of their Rivals for 3000 energy credits, both diverting their attention and potentially weakening that rival's military and economy. While raiding on behalf of an empire, Marauders are treated as allied and their systems can be travelled through. This form of raid cannot be bought off and while they will always reveal they were paid to raid, they will never tell by which empire. Marauders can only raid one empire at a time, but multiple Marauder empires can raid the same target. Marauders will never raid Fallen Empires and will always refuse to do so if asked.

Both forms of raiding involve the Marauders sending a fleet to that empire's territory. The Fleet will attack the nearest colonised Planet, destroying all outposts and mining stations along their path. It can and will travel through wormholes if the route is faster. A fleet on Marauding will go into the orbit of the target planet and either steal some resources depending on the amount of tribute demanded or killing up to 4 biological Pops. They will still be hostile on their way back. Fleet strength depends on the number of years passed, and thus facing them can result in a considerable challenge.

Empires can also permanently hire a Marauder Commander for 2000 Energy. Hired leaders are replaced if they die, start at level 3 and have the Mercenary Warrior trait.

Mercenaries

Once the mid-game year has been reached, the option to hire mercenary fleets of varying sizes will be unlocked. All players will be informed of this change. The chance for one Marauder empire to have mercenaries is rolled yearly, and 30 for each Empire/80 for nothing. Mercenary fleets cost a large Energy up-front payment, and consist of a fixed-size fleet that cannot be split, merged or disbanded, with a leader that cannot be reassigned. The fleet does not count towards Naval Capacity, but will only serve for a period of 5 years, after which the empire will have to renew their contract by paying the full cost again.

If a mercenary fleet is destroyed, it will be rebuilt and available for hire again within a year. If a Marauder Empire is destroyed while an empire is hiring their fleet, all mercenary fleets will cancel their contracts and join together to attack the vanquisher empire within 5 months. Marauder Refugees will also settle on the planets of the closest empire that has the Refugees Welcome policy.

The Horde

The Great Khan

3 years after the Mid-Game year starts, one of the Marauder empires might unify under a Great Khan, an expansionist but ultimately benevolent leader. The chance scales with the number of marauder empires in the galaxy. It can happen on its own at any point, however there is also a 2% chance for the event to be triggered each time a Void Dwelling is destroyed. If the game manages to reach the end game year without the event triggering, it can no longer occur naturally, but can still be triggered by destroying Void Dwellings.

Once this happens, the Marauder empire becomes a crisis faction called a Horde (labelled as a "Marauder Khanate") and will begin expanding in all directions, invading the systems of any empire that will not submit to the Khan, including other Marauder empires but excluding Fallen Empires. When the Horde forms, all hired marauder fleets and leaders will cancel their contracts and join the Horde.

At any time, it is possible for an independent empire that is not Genocidal to submit to the Khan and become a Satrapy subject. Becoming one will add a Void Dwelling and two marauder fleets to the capital system if settled, remove the empire from any federations they may be a part of, and subjects will become Satrapies as well. AI empires that lose more than two planets to the Horde have a yearly chance to submit, with the chance increasing the more planets are lost.

The main Horde fleet, called "Chosen of the Great Khan" is led by the Great Khan personally, a level 10 commander with the The Great Khan, Master Gunner, Artillerist II, Demolisher II, Gunship Focus II, Wrecker II and Reckless II traits. When the Great Khan's fleet is destroyed for the first time, they will escape the battle, returning within a year with a second fleet inside of a system they own. If the second fleet is destroyed again, the Khan will die, and the empire who dealt the killing blow will earn the Khan's Throne relic and the No Khan Do achievement. If the horde has been completely destroyed before the Khan makes their return, they are presumed dead after some time and will never return. If the Great Khan is not killed in battle, then after 20 years have passed since the rise of the Horde, The Khan may also die by disease or assassination. If neither of these happens, then the Khan will die a natural death between 15 and 45 years after the Horde was created.

The number of ships the Horde initially has depends on the game difficulty setting. The Horde will also create a new fleet every few years, the size of the fleet depending on the Naval Capacity provided by Satrapies.

If an empire recaptures a colonized system that belonged to another empire, it will get the option to return the system to its original empire for +40 Opinion and a Favor or keep control of it for −20 Opinion.

Available only with the Overlord DLC enabled.

Every 2 years, the Horde will rent all available fleets offered by mercenary enclaves. Each patron of a mercenary enclave will be warned about it and be given 60 days to rent its fleets so that the Horde cannot rent them. If the Great Khan dies all mercenary fleets will end their contracts.

Aftermath

Once the Great Khan dies, the Horde will become unreachable, leaving an automatic message if diplomatic contact is attempted. All of its fleets will retreat back to within the borders of the Horde's territory and stay there. After 200-300 days, if the Horde isn't destroyed, it will change into one of the following based on weighted chance:

  • If the Horde owns no planets, it will always turn back into a Marauder Empire. All Satrapies will become independent, and the Void Dwelling in the capital system is destroyed.
  • If the Horde owns between 1 and 5 planets, there are two potential outcomes:
    • 10 weight for staying united as a new Khanate empire, keeping all existing Horde fleets as well as all Satrapies, but destroying the Void Dwelling in the capital system. The empire's origin is set to Khanate Successor and it will have the Great Khan's Legacy civic, granting +10% Fire Rate.
    • 10 weight if there are more than 2 Satrapies, or 0 weight otherwise, for becoming a Fanatic Egalitarian and Xenophile Federation Builders regular empire.
      • All Satrapies will become independent, and the Void Dwelling in the capital system is destroyed. All fleets will transfer ownership to the Horde Successor State.
      • The Horde Successor State will then form a Galactic Union federation with every former Satrapy, with the Horde Successor State becoming its first president.
  • If the Horde owns more than 5 planets, an additional outcome is added on top of the previous two options:
    • 60 weight for splitting into a civil war. All Satrapies will become independent, and the Void Dwelling in the capital system is destroyed.
      • If the Horde owns between 6-9 planets, it will split into two regular empires with claims on each other's systems.
      • If the Horde owns more than 9 planets, it will split into four regular empires with claims on each other's systems.
      • In both cases, all empires will have the Diadochus civic, granting +15% Naval Capacity.

Heirs of the Khan interactions

Available only with the Nomads DLC enabled.

If a player empire has the Heirs of the Khan origin then the Horde is guaranteed to form. If the formation of the Horde is triggered by the destruction of a Void Dwelling, the player empire with the Heirs of the Khan origin that defeated most hunters will be offered to become the Great Khan first. If they refuse, the offer will be made to the next empire that defeated the most hunters. Empires that have the Defender of the Galaxy ascension perk or are Galactic Custodian or Galactic Emperor cannot receive this offer.

When the Horde would normally form, all Marauders will establish communications and will no longer be hostiles towards empires with the Heirs of the Khan origin, and those empires will be given a 10 year warning to arrive in one of the systems of the Marauder empire associated with their origin. The first empire with the origin that enters system will face a battle against a Marauders fleet. If for 3 years another empire with the Heirs of the Khan origin does not defeat the capital arkship, or there is no other empire with the Heirs of the Khan origin, the empire will receive the Khan's Throne relic and the following choices:

  • Leave the Marauders as they are.
  • Subjugate the Marauder empire associated with the origin and every 2 years, the empire has a 10% chance to receive Basic Resources or a Marauder fleet. Marauders can be contacted to stop or resume sending fleets.
  • Become the Horde. The empire will gain the Great Khan's Vision civic, Imposed Inclusion casus belli and control of all Marauder fleets. Only Satrapy subjects will be allowed and any other subject will be converted into one, but the empire will be able to demand subjugation regardless of Trust requirements. The empire will not be able to form new diplomatic agreements except Embassy. If the empire is part of a Federation or Galactic Community, it will leave them and cannot rejoin. If the empire is a subject, it will become independent. The Marauder empire associated with the origin will be subjugated but will not provide periodic rewards. This option is not available to empires that are Galactic Custodian or Galactic Emperor or have the Defender of the Galaxy ascension perk and the ascension perk will also become unavailable. Every year, the empire will receive the following reinforcements:
    • A construction ship if it has less than 5
    • 20 Assault Armies led by a level 3 Commander if it has less than 40
    • A fleet of 11 corvettes, 5 destroyers, 4 cruisers and a battleship led by a level 3 Commander with the Mercenary Warrior if total fleet power is less than 1000000.

If an empire with the Heirs of the Khan origin becomes the Horde, its ruler will gain the The Great Khan trait. If the ruler dies, the empire will receive the option to stop being the Horde, which will remove all aforementioned restrictions and grant independence to all subjects including the Marauders, but the latter will remain non-hostile and the Great Khan's Vision civic will be retained. Refusing will add −5 Monthly Loyalty from subjects.

If no empire with the origin manages to defeat the initial Marauder fleet in 10 years, the Horde will form as usual.

If the marauders are destroyed without triggering the khan event, you still will be able to become the khan, though you wont get the throne.

Hunters

Every 100-300 days, empires with the Heirs of the Khan origin have a 20% chance to be attacked by a Marauder fleet until they win the fight that starts when the Horde forms. The first time it happens, the origin empire will receive an option to gain for a year either 10% Ship Fire Rate or +10% Sublight Speed and +10% Hyper Jump Charge Time.

The first such fleet that is destroyed will grant 100-100000 Unity. Subsequent fleets have a 25% chance to offer to avoid combat in exchange for 200-5000 Energy or a duel where there is a 20% chance the Ruler will gain the Maimed trait and an 80% chance to avoid combat. Subsequent fleets also have a 25% chance to give the following when defeated:

  • 400 pops
  • Random resources
  • One of the hunter ships
  • Level 5 Commander with the Mercenary Warrior trait
  • Hunters will not attack for 6 years

The first hunter fleets are made up of corvettes. After 5 fleets have been destroyed, subsequent fleets will also include destroyers and after 10 fleets have been destroyed, they can include cruisers as well. The size of the fleets depends on game difficulty.

If an arkship is defeated by hunters, they will leave and the arkship will recover but the empire will lose resources depending on arkship tier:

  • 300 Operational Reserves and 75 Food and Alloys if arkship is tier 1
  • 1200 Operational Reserves and 300 Food and Alloys if arkship is tier 2
  • 2000 Operational Reserves and 500 Food and Alloys if arkship is tier 3

Ships

Both marauders and the horde use the same ship designs. Raiding fleets vary in size based on game year.
