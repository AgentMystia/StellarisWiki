Rise of the Manifesti is a colony event chain that has a 3% chance to trigger every year. It can only happen once per game. Uniquely among event chains Rise of the Manifesti can allow the creation of a new faction type.

Carnival Protestors
Protests have erupted across [colony name], driven by a growing anxiety over the perceived transformation of our society following first contact with an alien species. Demonstrators question long-standing traditions, challenge the foundations of governance, and demand adaptations to accommodate a future reshaped by this revelation.

Though non-violent, the sheer scale of these movements has placed a strain on civic services and disrupted daily life. However, many citizens do not see these protests as mere disorder. They see them as a reckoning, a sign that our very identity is being rewritten before our eyes.

The Manifesti
The protests on [colony name] have ceased almost as suddenly as they began. Many now point to The Manifesti, a shadowy collective known for provocative works that challenge societal norms. While their involvement remains unconfirmed, their latest projects have fixated on the alleged consequences of first contact, fueling both controversy and unrest.

Though the demonstrations have ended, The Manifesti remain. Their message spreads, their influence lingers. The time has come to decide how we respond.

Nipped in the Bud
The families of The Manifesti have been detained, and with them, the movement's leadership has been effectively neutralized. Yet, instead of compliance, the public's response has been one of outrage. Protests have grown fiercer, no longer confined to mere demonstrations but erupting into violent unrest.

A Thousand Words, None Official
A fabricated language has begun spreading through art centers and academic institutions, a deliberate rejection of the official tongue. Created by The Manifesti, its speakers refuse to acknowledge the legitimacy of our language, seeing it as a symbol of a society that can no longer define itself in the wake of first contact.

Officials warn that this growing refusal to communicate in established terms risks deepening divisions and destabilizing public discourse.

Mirrors, Brightly
The Manifesti have unveiled their most public exhibition to date. A new and extremely popular interactive experience on [world name] invites visitors to manipulate reflections and images of themselves, and to create distorted collages of political figures - some from other galactic empires.

Critics herald these pieces as casting a necessarily and harsh light upon our sense of self. The exhibition has also inspired a number of other satirical works both on domestic issues, and on pan-species relations.

Debate amongst our social researchers is lively; more conservative voices urge caution.
 
Trigger conditions:
  • A Thousand Words, None Official
 Mean time to happen:

145 days

A bold statement.

Rise of the Manifesti
Rumors spread that a new faction is emerging, driven by the ideals of 'Manifestism.' In the aftermath of first contact, as our understanding of the universe and our place within it comes into question, its proponents challenge established norms. They advocate for radical self-expression and a fundamental reevaluation of societal structures. Existing factions complain that their gatherings are being disrupted by Manifestist agitators, whose relentless challenges to governance are sowing discord across [capital name].
 
Trigger conditions:
  • Mirrors, Brightly
  • Gained rewards
 Mean time to happen:

155 days

While some decry the move as censorship, most now understand a simple truth. There are limits to free expression when the stability of the empire is at stake.

A Hush Descends
The buzz surrounding the Manifesti Faction has quitened, fading as quickly as the arrival of protests which started it. Some activists we spoke to claimed their motivations ranged from pure satire to conscientious objection.

Attendance at galleries and museums has never been higher, but the public gaze has already moved on to newer, controversial groups of artists. We will continue to sift through the data gathered during this strange moment in [founder species adjective] cultural history.
