Console:Species rights

Version

This article has been verified for the current console version (3.14) of the game.

Species rights determined how species are treated in an empire. The rights can be determined individually for each species and can be changed every 10 years. Default rights for new species in the empire can also be set, which can later be modified for individual species. What rights can be selected depends on whether the species is the main species of the empire and what ethics, civics, policies, and authority the empire has. The set rights interface can be found from the species panel.

When a species template is created, it inherits the species right of the unmodified species. However, the template can be given separate rights afterwards.

Citizenship

Citizenship represents the status of a species in an empire. It also affects pops' ability to seek refuge: empires can allow all refugees, granting them residence or full citizenship, or only accept refugees from species that already have full citizenship.

The primary species, as well as species from the same class if the empire has the Selective Kinship civic, always have Full Citizenship rights. Species with Full Military Service also have Full Citizenship if the empire has the Citizen Service civic.

Assimilation

Assimilation is a special citizenship type where pops are changed over time to match the primary species of the empire. Each year 3, 6, or 12 pops will be assimilated on each planet. The type of assimilation depends on the empire and the pop in question. Assimilation of biological species is not available to genocidal empires. Assimilating an invaded primitive species will remove stellar culture shock modifiers. Species under assimilation will not generate Crime.

  • Pops with the Hive-Minded trait cannot be assimilated by non- Hive Mind empires unless the trait can be removed.
  • Pops with the Psionic trait will lose it if assimilated to add the Cybernetic or Hive-Minded trait.
  • Pops with the Cybernetic trait will lose it if assimilated to add the Psionic trait.

Empires with a robotic primary species can also assimilate all robotic pops of different species. This will change their species to the primary species.

Living standards

Living standards represents how economically favored a species is, as well as the gap between different social strata. The higher the living standards of a Pop is, the more Consumer Goods it will use, but the happier it will be. Higher living standards also remove the penalties from unemployment. Happiness bonuses require the empire to not have a deficit of consumer goods.

Most living standards cannot be used for species with Undesirables citizenship unless their purge type is set to either Neutering or Synaptic Service.

Egalitarian standards

The following living standards are only available to Egalitarian empires. Empires that have the Shared Burdens or Worker Cooperative civics cannot use other living standards except Chemical Bliss and Non-Existent. Egalitarian standards affect all strata the same way and remove the penalties from unemployment.

Chemical Bliss

Chemical Bliss is a living standard available to all individualist empires for biological species after researching the Subdermal Stimulation technology unless the species has the Chattel Slavery or Domestic Servitude rights. It affects all strata with the following effects:

  • +40% Happiness
  • +0.5 Trade Value
  • +1 Consumer Goods Upkeep (+0.1 for slaves)
  • −60% Resources from Jobs
  • No unemployment penalties

Changing the living standards of a species from Chemical Bliss to anything else will give the entire species the Withdrawal Symptoms modifier, which reduces its happiness by −10% and its resource output by −20% for 5 years.

Military service

Military Service determines a species' right to join the police or military and whether or not they can become military leaders.

  • Primary species must always be set to Full Military Service
  • Xenophobe empires cannot set alien species to Full Military Service, unless they have the Selective Kinship civic, in which case they can use Full Military Service for species from their primary species class.
  • Robotic species can only be used for Military Service if the empire has the Artificial Specialists technology or a Machine primary species.

Migration controls

Migration controls affect both migration between planets within the empire and migration to and from empires with a Migration Treaty. Even if Migration Controls are disabled, enslaved pops can only auto-resettle if a Slave Processing Facility is built on the planet or a Transit Hub is built on a starbase in the system. If Migration Controls are enabled, the Slave Processing Facility and Transit Hub will not allow auto-resettlement. Migration controls do not affect manual resettlement.

Purge type

Purging type is only available to species with the Undesirables citizenship. It determines how quickly or humanely the pops are purged. Every option besides Neutering requires the Purge policy to be set to Allowed.

Pops being purged can produce resources based on the purge type, for as long as the pop has the Undesirables citizenship, not just when the pop is being declined, meaning that a planet with a pop of Processing purge type will yield +6, while a planet with 2 of these pops will yield +12 in total, until all undesirable pops have been purged. These monthly resources are modified by planet unique buildings in the same way as the associated jobs; for example an Energy Grid gives an additional +1 per pop under Chemical Processing.

The Fanatic Purifiers civic gives +2 unity for each pop being purged while the Devouring Swarm civic gives +2 society research per pop in addition to the resources said purge type may have.

Each planet has at most one pop at a time that is Declining; purging is not the only way it can happen, but it generally takes priority. A decline progress bar goes from 0 to 100, and fills up at a rate depending on the purge type. When the bar fills up, an undesirable pop on the planet will be removed. Depending on the purge type, the pop has a chance to be displaced as a refugee, if it is eligible; otherwise it is killed. Most purge types also cause other empires to get opinion penalties towards the purging empire, provided they have enough Intel to know it is happening.

  • All non-gestalt empires that have the same primary species as the purged pop will have their opinion of the purger reduced by −25 per pop, to a maximum of −1000.
  • All other empires will also have their opinion of the purger reduced by −5, and Egalitarian or Xenophile empires who don't allow species-wide purges have it reduced by a further −5–15 per pop, to a maximum of −1000.
