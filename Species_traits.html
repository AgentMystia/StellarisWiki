Species traits

Species traits represent a species' innate functions, abilities, and personality. Though traits are initially decided at a species level, individual pops of the same species can end up with different traits with genetic modification, producing new subspecies. Some traits affect how strongly a pop is attracted to certain factions. Hovering over a species' portrait will list the combined effects of all species traits.

Most traits come in mutually exclusive groups that affect the same aspect of the species, such as Migratory and Sedentary. These groups include traits with opposite effects, as well as stronger variants of a trait. The Adaptive Evolution situation can gives species traits that are mutually-exclusive however.

Pop modification

The template modification menu

A species' traits are selected at the start of a new game but those can be changed or improved as new technologies are researched. Both biological and robotic pops can be modified and each has its own set of traits for biological pops or robotic pops. In addition, a small number of events can modify the population of a colony.

Species cannot be modified if there is an ongoing ascension situation. Empires with the
Natural Design
Natural Design
Innate Design
civic also cannot modify biological species.

Number of traits

The number of traits that a species can have is limited by two values: trait points and trait picks. Additional trait points and trait picks can be obtained throughout the game from various sources.

  • Trait Points are spent on positive traits and added by negative traits. Traits that have both positive and negative effects usually do not cost points, nor do certain special traits. The starting number of trait points is 2 for biological species, 1 for Machine species, and 0 for Mechanical species.
  • Trait Picks is the total number of traits that a species may have. The starting number of Trait Picks is 5 for biological and Machine species and 4 for Mechanical species. Traits which do not cost Trait Points also do not cost Trait Picks.

Species templates

Population modification is done via templates, which creates a subspecies. Templates can be applied to pops per subspecies (including original species), per planet, or per subspecies on a planet. Modified pops are considered part of the same species as the original even if the name and portraits have been changed. They will inherit the same species rights as the original species but those can be then changed to something different for each subspecies.

Templates with zero current pops can be freely modified or deleted[1]. However, templates with at least one pop cannot be changed directly and the template modification window presents only the option to create a new one.

After a template has been applied, a special project to modify the selected pops appears in the situation log. The project is canceled in 30 days if not started. Depending on the type of modification, how severe the modification (total amount of trait point differences), and how many pops were picked, this special project can be very expensive in science. The system is currently designed towards favoring bulk modification of many traits over specific via a high base cost.

The cost to modify a species can be reduced by −50% by the following traditions: Synthetics tradition tree finisher, Advanced Tools, Modular Cybernetics or Allelic Expression. Additionally, the Plentiful Fossils archaeology site can add a permanent −25% modification cost.

A non-default subspecies can also be set in its species rights to passively integrate into the default subspecies over time.

Auto-modding

An auto-modded pop group with multiple applied traits, each at 100% efficiency

Certain traits grant the auto-modding effect, which will cause the trait to copy the bonuses of others traits that will help a pop in its job. The bonuses start at 0% and gradually increase towards 100% each month at a rate that depends on how many pops have to be auto-modded simultaneously. The speed is increased by pop assembly buildings. The effects of auto-modding are not shown in the Species menu but can be seen if a population group is selected and hovering over the auto-modding trait will display all copied effects and their current efficiency. If a game is started with an auto-modding trait, all pops will start fully optimized.

Notably, auto-modding effects are not limited to only applying a single trait effect on a Pop Group at a time. I.E. if a Pop Group is split into working multiple jobs then auto-modding will apply all eligible trait effects to the same Pop Group at the same time. Despite what the in-game description for Auto-Modding Traits currently says, these effects will all eventually scale to 100% efficiency, regardless of how many different jobs the Pop Group is working.[2]

There are six auto-modding traits that will copy the effects of traits that are beneficial to a pop's job. Each trait has its own set of traits it can simulate, thus while traits applied by auto-modding do not stack with their "regular" versions (e.g. Adaptive Frames' Harvesters & regular Harvesters), the traits applied by different auto-modding traits (e.g. Vocational Genomics' Agrarian & Adaptive Mutations' Adaptive Plows) will stack with each other.[3]

Uplifting

For pre-sapient species, the option to create templates is replaced with the option to uplift and bring them to sapience. It requires the Epigenetic Triggers technology and costs 3000 Society research. Upon completion, the empire instantly integrates the uplifted species and gains 50 Influence. In addition, the uplifted species gains +10% Happiness when living within the borders of the empire that uplifted it. The initial ethics of the uplifted species are determined by a series of initial events. Afterwards, the species is fully susceptible to ethics divergence.
